## Microphysics

(This is not as important but something Hikari would find interesting, and I've developed it, so I'm deciding to include it. There will not be a quiz!)

Hikari also finds a Valdan science node mostly concerned with measuring the xeon, figuring out how it works at a molecular level, and understanding how to color it certain ways.

> [!quote]
> Each particle (which acts both like a physical thing and as a wave) has a certain charge. It's charged a certain amount with each color, and the particular proportion of charges it has determines its particular color.
>
> When it comes in contact with physical things (such as stars, planets, life, gems, or life forms that can charge it at will) it becomes colored because of that interaction, at which point the particle of xeon both gains different characteristics and starts influencing the xeon around it (its color starts rubbing off on the particles around it).
>
> A particle is gray when it's colored more or less same in all colors. (e.g. R40 G40 B40, or R33 G32.5 B33.23) This happens because if there's nothing around to color it, all the particles' charges will rub off on each other and eventually it'll come to a place of equilibrium. Outer space is the most gray of all places, especially when there's nothing else around for lightyears. Air above a planet and within water or other liquids are also pretty good places for gray xeon, provided that their temperatures aren't too extreme.
>
> Charge is just how much color it has; say you have two particles:
>
> Particle1: R50 G0 B0
> Particle2: R5 G0 B0
>
> These two particles have the same color but different charges. So if Particle2 was charged more, it would enact the same effect as Particle1 would.

It goes on to say that different proportions of charges will produce certain effects, such as running the xeon through a ruby so that the xeon starts putting off heat, or through a sapphire so it puts off cold. There are many other substances that achieve different effects, and this all comes down to the exact proportions of charge that it requires.

The xeon is usually invisible when the charge isn't strong, and it usually becomes visible when it's charged.

It mentions how certain people can achieve the same effects as some of these gems, but how it isn't clear why some people can achieve these effects while others can't. It's surmised that it has something to do with the makeup of the xenic body of some people compared to others.

# Basic Astronomy

Hikari learns from a Valdan science node that the supercluster where everyone lives is much, much bigger than she imagined, that it contains around 10 quadrillion stars and is about a billion light years wide.

The supercluster is shaped like a 3D star, with a Center Group of stars and many many tendrils of red xeon flowing out from the Center Group. Stars, in turn, form along the red currents, and then explode and planets are formed from the matter ejected. Blue currents often form alongside the red currents as planets, asteroid belts, and other heavy, solid matter is formed.

## The Toloi Current

The Kotharak Field is located in the Ominidan Sector of the Toloi Current. The Ominidan Sector is most notable for containing the exozinik homeworld, and the the Toloi Current contains both human homeworlds and the grugg homeworld.

## Warprunning and Portals

The Toloi Current alone is vast, holding billions and billions of stars, and is generally navigated by warprunning, which requires a warp core, or by portal. Both of these methods rely on combining red and blue xeon to either partially enter the xenic plane (in the case of warprunning) or ripping a hole into the xenic plane outright (in the case of portals).

Warprunning can be multiple times faster than the speed of light, depending on the warp core and the thrusters, and portalling is instantaneous.

# Basic Valda Info

Valda is one of the human homeworlds, along with Ydilik. It has always been on the forefront of technology, and ages ago all of the natural plant and animal life on it was destroyed in favor of massive city sprawl that goes around almost the entire planet.

Hikari sees in the Valdan histories that she reads a huge disregard for old myths and historical records from before this shift. Their focus has been on technological achievement, capitalistic growth, and imperialism.

Many of the most influential organizations, whether they be corporations or mobs, are run from the dangerous, seductive mess that is Valda.

## Krangor

Krangor Tech is one of the superpowers of the universe, a vastly powerful, wealthy, and dangerous corporation that has a colonial hand in almost every corner of the supercluster. It's run by Ulysses Krangor who created it several hundred years ago.

Their tech is sturdy, powerful, and blocky, and the specialize in military ships, mechs, robots, and anything else you can think of.

## Ailith

Ailith is right up there with Krangor as one of the superpowers, but their approach has been different. They specialize in expensive, sleek cybermods, and their message is extremely transhumanistic: they want people as a whole to merge with technology and become something new.
Ailith is run by Uli Ailith, a full cyborg who is worshipped by some as a god incarnate.
They make the best consumer robots and are known for their advanced general AI.

## Basic Mechanics

She reads how copper conducts xeon and silicon is a xeon semiconductor, so people have figured out how to make xeon circuits and chips.

As far as big moving machines go, its usually sunstone propulsion for thrusters, pistons, and any moving parts. There's a ruby core that converts the ambient xeon around it into red xeon, and then wires and sometimes tubes that go to all the parts that need to move.

In tougher, more expensive stuff titanium will be used to make it sturdier, jasper will be used to decrease friction, and aluminum will be used to make the whole thing lighter.

## The Gray Web

Hikari stumbles upon a Valdan node that seems to not have been edited in a while that details the history of the gray web.

She reads that it was actually discovered, not created, by Valdan scientists. It is a massive network of gray xeon that connects the entire supercluster. Scientists surmise that it was created by an extinct species to house massive hoards of knowledge, though they don't know how to access that knowledge.

To connect to the gray web, one must have access to a nexus, which hooks into the web itself, and then a terminal, by which you navigate the web nodes. Many ships have their own nexuses, though some nodes are easier to access through certain nexuses.

Many nexuses will provide search engines or searchable databases of links of nodes to make it easier for users to find the nodes they want.

# The Ancient Ones

Hikari reads a couple books detailing an ancient species called the Ancient Ones. They created massive, powerful stone technology and seemed to last for thousands of thousands of years before getting completely wiped out somewhere around 10,000 years ago. No one knows what wiped them out.

Much of today's tech was inspired by remnants of their tech. No one has been able to decode their language so far.

# Intro

The [[Xeon Rapture Universe Overview|Xeon Rapture Universe]] was built from the ground up with collaborative storytelling in mind. It includes:
- Many different genres so there's something for everyone.
- A big magic/technology system that works everywhere in the universe.
- Tons of options for customizing characters including:
	- a wide variety of species, all with their different features and proclivities
	- a bunch of classes, all enabling you to gain power through different means, such as powers, technology, magic, or spirits
	- a rough leveling system that makes it easy to keep track of your character's power level

The worldbuilding isn't highly detailed *on purpose* so that you can help me fill in all the details!

Xeon Rapture Storytelling is a simple rundown of all the information you'll need to make a character (or several, or many) in order to get started telling your own stories in the Xeon Rapture Universe! It's basically just a simplified, story-focused version of the XRRPG, so if you're already familiar with that then the XRST won't be a huge leap.

Below you'll find a guide to creating your character, some basic rules for the XRST, a simple rundown on the XRU, and some other guidelines and resources.

# Character Creation

Check out the [[XRST Character Creation Template]] for an easy-to-use template for character creation, and see [[XRST Characterization Tips]] for if you need some guidance.

Below are the basic steps for creating a character in the XRU. Consider them suggestions, if you find a better way or want to paint outside the numbers go for it! As long as you're keeping to the logic of the XRU anything is acceptable.

## 1) Aesthetic or Vibe

Decide what aesthetic or vibe you want for your character! 

- Do you want a heroic, strong type? A smarter, trickier one? Maybe a straight-up villain?
- What role does this character play in the stories you want to tell? What kinds of abilities will make them interesting for that role?
- What genres do you want this character to frequent? Are they fantasy-only, sci-fi-only, horror-focused, a mix of many genres?
- What motivates them, and how do these motivations influence how they talk, what kind of choices they make, who they associate with?
- How brutal do you want your stories to be? Should your character be able to handle horror and trial, or do they work better in more heroic, less morally-gray situations?

## 2) Species

Pick a Species from the [[XRST Basic Info#Mortal Species|list below]].

## 3) Chroma

Pick a Chroma from the [[XRST Basic Info#Xeon|list below]]. This determines what color of xeon you'll be able to manipulate if you pick an Adept class.

- Certain species are more likely to have a certain chroma.
- Your character may have multiple chromas, but it's very very rare and growing up with more than one chroma can lead your character to getting ostracized or accidentally hurting someone or themselves with your uncontrolled powers.

## 4) Character Level

Decide what level your character will be.

- Level isn't essential to building a character but can be a good way of keeping track of how powerful and experienced your character is.
- Gain a class level each time your character levels up (e.g. a 6th level character may have 3 levels in Warrior and 3 levels in Swordmaster). Most classes only have 6 levels so if you end up levelling past that you'll have more than one class.
- You can level up whenever you feel like your character has earned a new ability! Can be triggered by a training period, a big realization or character growth, or someone teaching you how.
- Your character is able to channel xeon for longer and take more hits the higher level they are! Also, all their abilities are stronger and the damage they deal more substantial.

Below is a chart showing the general level-up progression.

| Char. Level | Power Level                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 0           | Just a civilian, easy to overpower, intelligence not far above average, can't protect themselves against supernatural threats.                                                                                                                                            |
| 1           | More powerful than a civilian but not by too much; very inexperienced and unschooled in how things work.                                                                                                                                                                  |
| 2           | Becoming skilled in their abilities, not super experienced in the world but getting better.                                                                                                                                                                               |
| 3           | Very competent in their xenic abilities and normal skills but overall power and experience has not caught up.<br>A group of people at this level can take on a strong, elite-tier enemy together.                                                                         |
| 4           | Overall power and experience is growing, starting to understand how everything fits together. Skills and abilities are diversifying, and that experience in different fields brings greater cognizance and foresight.                                                     |
| 5           | Fairly experienced adventurer or operative at this point, can go toe-to-toe with most people one-on-one. Getting fairly able to deal with supernatural threats.                                                                                                           |
| 6           | Very experienced overall, power level is quite high.<br>A group of people at this level can take on an immortal, wyvern, or mech together.                                                                                                                                |
| 7           | Becoming a master in their fields, and begins to diversify even more.                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| 8           | Extremely proficient in many things, becoming a legend if anybody knows their name. Experience is prodigious.                                                                                                                                                             |
| 9           | A legend, almost impossible to beat by mortal means (one-on-one at least), extremely experienced and powerful. <br>Still unable to take on an immortal alone, and any god will annihilate them.<br>A group of these people could take on a dragon or lesser god together. |
| 10+         | For each level beyond 9 one gets closer and closer to immortal-level power and experience, though whether they'll be able to get there is another matter.                                                                                                                 |

## 5) Class(es)

Pick a Class or two (see [[XRST Basic Info#Classes|here]] for a list).

- Decide which abilities from each class you want. Which ones will fit best with your character aesthetic and personality? Which ones make your character more interesting?
- Feel free to modify your class abilities or aesthetics as you see fit. Get creative! Give yourself abilities that match the mechanism of your power. 
- The classes below give you an assortment of abilities, but as long as you keep the general spirit and capabilities of the class you can get very creative. The classes are suggestions!
- Most classes have a max level of 3, so to go above character level 3 you'll have to grab more than one class.
- Keep in mind that as you channel the xeon or use class abilities it tires your character out! Even if you're just using the tech from a technologist class, that kind of focus is hard to maintain for long periods of time.

## 6) Characterization

Flesh out the following characterization info:

- Name
- Age
- Gender
- Appearance
- Personality
- Motivations/Goals
- Backstory
- Connections

## 7) Inventory

Choose some items to put in your inventory (inventory resources coming soon).

# Basic Xeon Rapture Lore

## Xeon

Xeon is the basic spiritual energy of the universe and is utilized by the gods to create planets, stars, and living beings. It's harnessed by mortals in their magic, technology, and powers, and is the main way they gain power over the environment and other mortals. All spirits, whether they be demons, elemental spirits, or mortal spirits, are made of xeon.

Every person in the XRU has a chroma, which is the color of xeon their spirit is mostly made of. Your chroma determines what kinds of powers you have available to you.

Here are the colors of xeon and their attributes:

| Gray       | Intelligence                     | Everywhere else<br>Interstitial areas (space, etc.) | -                          | Learning, innovation, recall, synthesizing data |

## Gods and Mortals

The gods of the XRU are multitudinous, each laying claim to a different dominion and situated in their respective pantheon. These gods fight amongst themselves, create mortal species and other lifeforms, learn more about the universe, and enact their will upon the environment and other people.

There are both light and dark gods, none of which are omnipotent or omniscient. The light gods are generally good but are not perfect, and have squabbles and grudges just like the mortals do (think Greek or Norse gods).

Each species of mortal was created by a different god!

## Reference

Below is a list of reference documents that explain certain things you can do in the XRU. 

These are documents straight from the XRRPG, so if you want a quick rundown on XRRPG mechanics click [[Quick Start Guide#Basic Rules|here]].

- [[Combining Tech Gadgets]]: If you take multiple technologist classes you can combine the gadgets you make! 
	- Feel free to combine drones and swords and drones and brawler tech if you wish, the restrictions on those combinations only really matter in the XRRPG.
- [[Cybermods]]: Here is a list of cybermods you can have!
- [[Spirit Decay]]: You can choose to push your limits and channel more xeon than is healthy for your spirit and body, and if you do that your spirit will incur Spirit Decay.
- [[Spirit Anchors]]: A device you can have implanted in the base of your skull that grants functional immortality (with some side effects). It does *not* allow you to live too much past your normal lifespan, however, just makes it way harder to 100% kill you.
- [[Xenic Substances]]: When you channel the xeon through certain substances the xeon will turn a different color and enact certain mystical effects; this is how technology was created to utilize the xeon. Use these substances to create new devices or otherwise manipulate the xeon!
- [[Drug Rules]]: In this resource is a list of cyberpunk drugs and their effects; feel free to use these or make some of your own! This list will include magic drugs at some point as well.
- [[XRMECHA (Kids in Mechs)]]: Here are the rules for building and piloting a mech! There are a lot of rules so I'll probably make a simplified version sometime in the future but for now just go and try to figure out the mech rules that I already have.
- [[Spaceship Rules]]: Similar to Mech Rules, just some mechanics for being in and piloting a spaceship.
- [[Webrunning 2.0]]: Rules for hooking your mind up to the gray web and running through it! Very cyberpunk, go wild.

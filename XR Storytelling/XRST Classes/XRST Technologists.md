# Gunslinger

You gain a general proficiency with all technology that increases as your character level increases.

You are able to create any of the gadgets you use in this class as long as you can find scrap to make them. Otherwise you might have to buy parts, which will cost 30 Valdan credits per gadget (deduct the original price if you're modifying an existing gadget).

Some gadgets are upgraded versions of lower-level gadgets, and with those devices they can perform the abilities of their lower-level counterparts.

| Class Level | Gadget Max Tier | Stamina Cost | Details                                                                                             |
| ----------- | --------------- | ------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1           | I               | 1            | You can make all the Tier I guns. Each gun can have 1 effect.<br>Each gun can have up to 2 effects. |
| 2           | II              | 2            | You can make all the Tier II guns.                                                                  |
| 3           | III             | 3            | You can make all the Tier III guns.<br>Each gun can have up to 3 effects.                           |

You can create your own guns! Guns do half damage if you're out of their range. Rifle, musket, and sniper projectiles speed up after they leave the barrel, giving them both a minimum and a maximum range.

As you level up each gun can contain multiple effects, which you can switch between at will.

# Brawler

You gain a general proficiency with all technology that increases as your character level increases.

You are able to create any of the gadgets you use in this class as long as you can find scrap to make them. Otherwise you might have to buy parts, which will cost 30 Valdan credits per gadget (deduct the original price if you're modifying an existing gadget).

Some gadgets are upgraded versions of lower-level gadgets, and with those devices they can perform the abilities of their lower-level counterparts.

Brawlers can make gauntlets, armor, and boots that they use to deal damage, move quickly, defend themselves, and achieve other effects. Unlike other Techie classes, Brawlers create one full set of equipment that can achieve several effects, and have to remake the suit entirely to change what they can achieve.

The suit doesn't have to cover you entirely but should have a hand element, a foot element, and some armor element. Whether you have a helmet is an entirely aesthetic choice.

At level 1 you choose two effects total, and as you level up you can put more and more effects into your suit at one time. You only know how to install a certain amount of effects, and you must take 30 min and have your tools available in order to switch effects.

| Class Level | Total Effects Installed | Total Effects Known |
| ----------- | ----------------------- | ------------------- |
| 1           | 4                       | 6                   |
| 2           | 6                       | 8                   |
| 3           | 8                       | All                 |

You can now make emitters, which are small devices that you can throw which will stick to floors, walls, or ceilings. These devices emit shields and other effects, and can also be activated and kept on your person.

These devices remain tethered to you with an invisible xenic tether, and when the device is spent or when you're done with it you can make it come back to you.

You emitters can be destroyed, but as you level up you find better ways to armor them so they're harder to destroy.

You can either throw these by hand or launch them from a gun that you make!

# Dronemaster

You are able to create any of the gadgets you use in this class as long as you can find scrap to make them. Otherwise you might have to buy parts, which will cost 30 Valdan credits per gadget (deduct the original price if you're modifying an existing gadget).

Some gadgets are upgraded versions of lower-level gadgets, and with those devices they can perform the abilities of their lower-level counterparts.

You can now build drones! At the start you can have up to 2 drones active at any one time, and they will usually float behind you. As you level up, you will gain the ability to add different abilities to your drones.

Drones can float as little as a couple of inches off the ground. The max height of a drone goes up as your level does.

Drone Range means how far the drone can be from you and still stay connected. Drones that shoot have a beam range of 30 ft.

Your drones can be destroyed, but they become harder to hit and more heavily armored as you level up.

You can have up to 1 drone docked on a backpack at any one time.

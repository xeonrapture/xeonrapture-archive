# Intro

Spirit decay is what happens when you push your xenic spirit too hard, and a side-effect of pushing too far past your limits.

Also called xenic decay! Especially in techie places.

People who can see spirits or has xeon sensing notice your aura change when you have spirit decay, and there can be other physical effects depending on how far gone you are.

# Gaining Spirit Decay

You get spirit decay either by dying and getting recorped, by pushing your powers beyond your stamina, or by spending too long in a place that'll give you spirit decay.

Magic users who try to wield menon have a high risk of incurring xenic decay, and it's easy to get it from evil areas and cursed items.

Every time you incur spirit decay the worse it gets, and even if you cleanse the spirit decay the actual underlying spiritual effect doesn't go away; the next time you get spirit decay you go up to the subsequent level on the table below. Cleansing only removes *effects*, not the actual decay, so make sure you're tracking both your total spirit decay and you uncleansed spirit decay effects.

Some powerful beings can do a sort of **deep clean**, resetting your spirit and getting rid of all your spirit decay permanently! They gotta be *powerful* though, like Lower God level at least.

> [!NOTE] Note
> These mechanics are for the XRRPG game; if you're writing a story, feel free to be more flexible with the effects of spirit decay and how many levels you can get before dying!
> 
> In general just make sure spirit decay is incurred when recorping, pushing your xenic abilities past what you usually can do, and playing with [[Xeon#The Three Taboos|xenic taboos]]; and that there's some sort of increasing negative effect the more spirit decay you get.
> 
> In the end, spirit decay does end in unavoidable death! But feel free to play with when and where and how that happens.

## \*Extreme Decay

Roll a d10. 

- 10: Take the [[Critical Injuries#Major Curse|Major Curse]] Critical Injury.
- 9-2: You instantly fall unconscious and are subject to the [[Critical Injuries#More Intense|Withering]] Critical Injury as your spirit attempts to leave your body. 
- 1: Deal 3d8 damage to all those in a 10 ft radius circle around you and 2d8 damage to all those in a 50 ft radius circle as your body and spirit combust. Roll another d10.
	- 10: Your body ends up mostly intact but very badly burned: fall unconscious, take the [[Critical Injuries#Bleeding Out|Bleeding Out]] Critical Injury, and start doing death saves.
	- 9-4: Your body is disintegrating: fall unconscious, take the [[Critical Injuries#Necrosis|Necrosis]] Critical Injury, and start doing death saves.
	- 3-2: A part of your body is blown off. Take the [[Critical Injuries#Maimed|Maimed]] Critical Injury.
	- 1: You're vaporized, flesh and spirit alike.

If you die of any of the Critical Injuries you sustain from Extreme Decay your body incinerates into dust and your spirit disintegrates into pure xeon. Your spirit anchor is destroyed.

# Getting Rid of Spirit Decay

It gets harder and harder to really get the spirit decay out the more you get. There are a couple of ways to deal with it.

## Suppressing

Suppressing spirit decay effects removes the effects of the decay **temporarily**. It can be a good idea, however, when the effects get really bad and you need to function until you can really take care of the decay itself.

Certain items can suppress the effects of spirit decay temporarily, as can certain powerful machines, or potent tech consumables.

The absolute WORST way is to suppress only the physical effects with drugs and chemicals; this has the potential to harm your body and *also* not really get rid of the effects.

## Cleansing

Certain classes can cleanse the effects of spirit decay, but the higher the level of xenic decay you have the crazier the methods you have to go to to expunge the effects (see the table above for more info).

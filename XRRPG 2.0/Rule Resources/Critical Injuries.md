# Intro

So normally when you go down (e.g. drop to 0 HP) you're only incapacitated with no threat of death. BUT if you ever go down with a Critical Injury then you start doing death rolls! Critical Injuries are Injuries so severe that they put your life in jeopardy if not tended to.

Whenever you take a Critical Injuries, whatever hit you will probably leave a scar of some sort (provided you survive your death saves). Think about what kind of scar your character would have, physical or mental, and what it would look like and how you could integrate it into your character's conception of themselves.

Do Critical Injuries checks at the top of the team turn.

# Stabilizing

If you succeed on your death rolls then your Critical Injury goes away and you gain 1 HP. You still stay unconscious, however!

Being healed by a teammate while you're doing death saves brings you back to consciousness but you immediately lose Stamina equal to a quarter of your Max Stamina. If you ever hit 0 Stamina this way then you take the Stamina Exhaustion Critical Injury and have to succeed on a Exhaustion DC of 15 or go unconscious and start death rolls ([[#Stamina Exhaustion|see below]]).

A teammate can attempt to stabilize you by doing a mental Action (using Wit or Int) against either a DC 10 Action (for Normal Critical Injuries) or a DC 15 Action (for Intense ones).

It takes a good meal and a full night of sleep to heal one Critical Injury, and if you have multiple you must take multiple days to heal those Injuries.

# Normal

## Bleeding Out

Roll a d10 each turn:
- Every roll 1-5 counts as a failure and every roll 6-10 counts as a success. 
- Three failures = you die, and three successes = you're stable!

## Blunt Trauma

Roll 3d6 every turn: 
- 1s count as failures and 6s count as successes! 
- Three failures = you die, and three successes = you're stable.

# Stamina Exhaustion

## Quick Reference

- Gain this Critical Injury automatically when you run out of Stamina
- Exhaustion DC
	- When you take this Critical Injury you have to succeed on the Exhaustion DC (starts at 15) with your main Stat +1d Mastery Die, if you fail you go unconscious and are stable unless you have another Critical Injury you have to roll death rolls for
	- If you succeed you stay conscious and you can keep spending Stamina to go negative; every 5 you spend past 0 you roll the Exhaustion DC again to stay up
	- Exhaustion DC = 15 + your Stamina below 0, Exhaustion DC triggers every 5 you go negative
- Healing
	- To bring you back up a teammate has to succeed on your Exhaustion DC
	- If you ever have Stamina Exhaustion *plus another* Critical Injury when you go down only the death rolls for that Critical Injury matter, and succeeding on them stabilizes you (so if you ever fail the Exhaustion DC roll then you go unconscious and are immediately doing death rolls)
	- Goes away with a Deep Rest
	- Can't die from Stamina Exhaustion

## Details

If you ever run out of Stamina you take the Stamina Exhaustion Critical Injury!

The only Critical Injury that goes away with a Deep Rest. You *can't die* from the Stamina Exhaustion Critical Injury.

When you take this Critical Injury you have to Succeed on the Exhaustion DC, which starts as a Kinda Easy (DC 15) Action using your main Stat +1d Mastery Die. If you fail on this Action you go unconscious immediately and are stable unless you have another Critical Injury that makes you start doing death rolls.

After you spend another 5 Stamina you now have to Succeed on a Hard (DC 20) Action to stay awake, and the DC goes up by 5 for every 5 Stamina you spend past your Stamina Max. The Exhaustion DC is always 15 + how much negative Stamina you have.

If anybody tries to heal you or does any sort of action to bring you back after you go unconscious from this Critical Injury then *they* have to Succeed at the Action that you just Failed in order to bring you back up (e.g. you failed a Tricky DC 30 Action to stay awake, so you go unconscious, and your ally wants to bring you back up so now *they* have to beat a Tricky DC 30 Action to bring you back to consciousness). 

If you're down, nothing can bring you back to consciousness but a successful Action against the Exhaustion DC to bring you back or you finishing a Deep Rest, which refills your Stamina and clears this Critical Injury.

If you are ever given Stamina then this Critical Injury doesn't go away, but if it brings you above 0 Stamina then you come back to consciousness. If you ever hit 0 Stamina again, though, you start the checks against the Exhaustion DC over again starting at a DC 15 roll.

If you have both Stamina Exhaustion and another Critical Injury and you go down for any reason (e.g. fail the Exhaustion DC check or hit 0 HP) you *only* have to resolve your death rolls in order to stabilize, and if you're ever healed then you come back to consciousness.

# Physical Exhaustion

## Details

- Can't be healed by regular healing sources
- Comes into effect when you stay up too long or haven't eaten or drank water in too long (or any other environmental effect that wears on you after a while, such as extreme heat or cold)
- Goes away when you sleep, eat, or hydrate (or whatever else caused the issue)
- Can have multiple instances of this Injury for different sources of exhaustion

## Death Saves

Have to crit on a d12.

Every turn you roll the d12 the number you die at creeps up, starting at 1, then moving to 2, then to 3, etc. 

If you ever roll at or below the death number then you die, and if you ever roll a 12 you're stable, just unconscious.

# Intense

## Bleeding Out FAST

Roll a d10 each turn: 
- Every roll 1-5 counts as a failure and every roll 6-10 counts as a success. 
- TWO failures = you die, and three successes = you're stable!

## VERY Blunt Trauma

Roll 3d6 every turn: 
- 1s count as failures and 6s count as successes! 
- TWO failures = you die, and three successes = you're stable.

## Withering

Have to crit on a d8.

Every turn you roll the d8 the number you die at creeps up, starting at 1, then moving to 2, then to 3, etc. 

If you ever roll at or below the death number then you die, and if you ever roll an 8 you're stable, just unconscious.

## Necrosis

Have to crit on a d8, and if that doesn't work you try on a d10 next turn, then on a d12 the turn after that, and finally a d20.

If you fail on a d20 you die.

## Maimed

Use this for a deep, deep wounds or getting crushed by something massive.

Maimed is the rare Critical Injury in which there isn't a huge chance of dying.

Roll a 1d6. If you don't get surgical medical attention within 1d4 hours the results are permanent (and for a 1 you're dead). 
	- 6=right leg maimed
	- 5=left leg maimed
	- 4=right arm maimed
	- 3=left arm maimed
	- 2=spine broken
	- 1=head broken

## Major Curse

Oof.

Per day your max HP goes down by 1. If it ever reaches 0 you die.

You don't do death saves if you're knocked out with the Major Curse Critical Injury; you can only die it by having your max HP reduced to 0.

Cannot be removed by anything but the curse being cleansed, either by doing a [[Cleanse|ritual]], finding someone powerful and skilled who can cleanse it (by spiritual or technological means), or finding a holy place or spiritual being that can take it away. 

What will be required to cleanse the curse will depend on the origins and power of the curse itself.

## Disease and Poison

### Deterioration

You are immediately stable, but incapacitated. Do a 1d4 roll per hour, and on 3 1s you die. You must find a cure or other intense medical attention!

### Deep Disease

Roll a 1d4, on a 1 you instantly die, otherwise you're fine, the Critical Injury goes away, and just unconscious until your healed.

### Brutal Poison

You remain conscious but in immense pain. You must make an End Action against the poison or disease itself every 10 minutes.

The disease or poison has a minimum of 20 Difficulty (or higher if it's an even nastier poison) and you take 1 point of Poison on Success, 2 on Mixed Success, and 3 on Failure (you remove 1 point of Poison on Strong Success) . As soon as the Poison wraps around your health again, you die.

You must find a cure, have someone cure you, or get the poison out somehow.

# Death Blows

These are for wraparound dmg, when you get knocked unconscious and the leftover dmg would destroy entirety of your HP again, for Failing on a super risky Action, or getting destroyed by something extremely powerful and dangerous like a god.

## Demolished

For physical sources of dmg.

Roll 1d20. 
- On a 20 you suffer the Blunt Trauma Critical Injury above. 
- On a 19 you survive but permanently maimed (roll on maim table). 
- On 2-18 you instantly die. 
- On a 1 you instantly die *and* your spirit anchor is also destroyed. 

## Obliterated

For xenic sources of dmg.

Roll 1d10
- On a 10 you suffer the Blunt Trauma Critical Injury above. 
- On 2-9 you are instantly vaporized. 
- On a 1 you are instantly vaporized *and* your spirit anchor is also destroyed.

## Disintegrated

For insane amounts of dmg, gods deciding to unmake you, that sort of stuff.

Roll a 1d100.
- On a 100 you suffer the Maimed Critical Injury above. 
- On all other numbers you and your spirit anchor are completely destroyed.

# Intro

You wake up to an avalanche of color, dizzying, moving so fast you feel like you're flying, body buoyant and stomach in your throat. 

You suddenly land, surrounded by soaring, blue-leafed trees and standing in a river of crystal, bright pink sky searing above you. Next to you a whale-like creature breaches, three red eyes considering you calmly and gills clasping at the air.

A pinprick of light nears your face and coalesces into a tiny blue ghost, little face scrunched up like it's trying to get a look at you. You wave it away and it darts off, sticking its tongue out at you.

You step out of the river and are suddenly completely dry. You start your journey.

For what feels like thousands of miles you walk, footsteps on sand, through grass, into mud and atop the mountains. You meet more spirits, some friendly, some ugly and mean, most uninterested. You see sights you couldn't have imagined, accomplish things unbelievable, learn and push the limits of your belief.

At last you meet Deno, Primordial God of Dreams, who opens your mind to you and teaches you how to taste the color of the night sky, smell the footsteps of the fox on the trail ahead of you, see the odor of a strong, cold rain. You are unmade and remade in all shapes, feeling every particle, one with the universe and yet apart. Your true eye is open and you experience all.

At last you awake. Your bed is beneath you, your body as solid as ever, the sun peeking through the window.

A sigh. All a dream.

---

The Xenic Plane is a dreamworld that's influenced heavily by the psyches of those that inhabit and travel through it. It is the spiritual world of the Xeon Rapture Universe, existing parallel to the physical realm, and is inhabited by gods and spirits, devils and demons. In the Xenic Plane, the only things limiting you are the strength of your belief and the word of the gods.

Click [[xr/Worldbuilding/Lore/The Xenic Plane|here]] for a detailed explanation of the Xenic Plane, how it works, and what it should feel and look like.

# What to Bring to the Table

Make sure you bring the following to a session in the Xenic Plane:

- An internet-connected device to use for rules and/or your character sheet (like a laptop, phone, tablet, etc.)
- A pre-existing XRRPG character sheet (feel free to use the [[XRRPG 2.0 Core Rules#Character Creation|online character creator]])
- A bunch of d8 (max like six)
- Google doc or pencil and paper for you character sheet if you'd like
- Snacks!
- Drinks!
- Ambiance (dreamy music, ethereal lighting, whatever you need to get in the mood)

In addition to all the physical things you need to bring, come ready to abandon the logic of the physical world and embrace a spiritual realm where anything is possible.

# Character Creation

In the Xenic Plane your character is an extension of an existing XRRPG character, so make sure you have an existing XRRPG character sheet available for reference.

Here's what you'll end up writing down for a character sheet in the Xenic Plane:

- Experience Gained
- Base Dice Pool (from 1d8-3d8)
- Basic Acts (level 1-3)
	- Includes three Basic Acts that everyone gets, **Willpower**, **Hesitate**, and **Help**
- Special Acts (level 1-3)
- Health (half of your XRRPG HP)

# Belief

As you travel through the xenic plane you will come across hurdles that seem absolutely impossible to overcome without the powers and abilities you've picked up in the physical world. 

This realm, however, is not bound by silly restrictions such as the laws of physics but only by your expectations and the rules of the xeon around you.

For every difficult thing you do in the xenic plane you will roll a certain amount of d8s and depending on the result of those dice you generate an amount of Belief.

| Roll | Meaning     | Resulting<br>Belief |
| ---- | ----------- | ------------------- |
| 1    | Doubt       | -1                  |
| 2-4  | Expectation | 0                   |
| 5-7  | Hope        | +1                  |
| 8    | Confidence  | +2                  |

To accomplish what you want to accomplish you must believe that you can! A task that is made quite a bit harder by the expectations you've built up over a lifetime of living in the physical world.

The more achievable an action *seems* to be the easier it is to hit the requisite amount of Belief, and the more unachievable the harder it is. The more you've achieved in real life the more confidence you have in your ability to achieve what you set your mind to, and the easier you'll find believing that you can in the xenic realm as well.

## Spirit Decay

If a character has active, non-cleansed spirit decay, take the level of spirit decay, divide it by 2, round up, and subtract that *in addition to* the -1 when you roll Doubt!

> [!NOTE]
> Spirit Decay Penalty = - (active spirit decay level ÷ 2, rounded up)

## Belief Results

| Belief<br>Rolled | Believability of Act | Meaning                                                                                                                                                            |
| ---------------- | -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| ≤-1              | Mundane*             | Outcome is worse than expected, a fumble; whoever did this must've been overcome with uncertainty and gotten into their own head (or inexperienced to begin with). |
| 0                | Reality              | Business as usual. What you would expect the waking world to feel like if no one had xenic powers.                                                                 |
| 1-2              | Strange              | If this happened in the real world you'd swear there was something magical was going on.                                                                           |
| 3-4              | Bizarre              | Very weird. In reality this would be *impossible* without a xenic explanation.                                                                                     |
| 5-6              | Supernatural         | Something only someone very practiced in their xenic powers could pull off.                                                                                        |
| 7-8              | Implausible          | Almost impossible even *with* powers.                                                                                                                              |
| 9-10             | Incredible           | What is happening strains credulity so far you're having a hard time believing your eyes, dreamworld or not.                                                       |
| 11+              | Unimaginable         | You literally cannot imagine this happening.                                                                                                                       |

- \*There's no mechanical penalty for rolling negative Belief, just a suggestion to integrate it into your storytelling: your character might start spiraling, have a crisis of confidence, or approach similar checks with some trepidation in the future.

## Experience

As you roll to overcome hurdles you'll gain Experience which you can use to upgrade your Acts. You get Experience every time you roll to achieve anything, regardless of whether you succeed, and the amount of Experience you get per check is equal to the amount of Belief you roll ignoring any Doubt.

| Act<br>Level | Experience <br>Required <br>to Upgrade |
| ------------ | -------------------------------------- |
| -1->0        | 10                                     |
| 0->1         | 15                                     |
| 1->2         | 30                                     |
| 2->3         | 45                                     |

## Base Dice Pool

You start with a dice pool based on what your Level is in the real world (which correlates with your overall competence and confidence in yourself). 

To do anything in the xenic plane you'll roll your Dice Pool—which equals your Base Dice Pool plus any extra dice if teammates are helping you and/or you're good at what you're doing—count up the Belief generated, and compare that against how much Belief is required for the task at hand.

| XRRPG <br>Level | Base <br>Dice Pool |
| --------------- | ------------------ |
| 1-3             | 1d8                |
| 4-6             | 2d8                |
| 7-9             | 3d8                |

# Acts

Whenever you try to undertake anything hard you'll be working an Act: Basic Acts include several different skills all wrapped into one and Special Acts are special abilities you get depending on what your classes are in the real world.

When you do an Act you'll roll your Base Dice Pool plus an amount of extra d8 equal to how many levels you have in that Act.

## Basic Acts

When you first enter the xenic plane you gain Stat Points to spend on Acts equal to your stats in the physical realm. When spending Points they must go into an Act that matches their Stat, and each Point you spend on a Basic Act increases its level by one. Each Basic Act has a level max of 3.

If you have any negative stats you must put that negative Point into an Act. When rolling an Act with a negative level, either subtract 1d8 from your Base Dice Pool or, if you would roll 0d8, roll 2d8 and only count the lowest result!

> [!NOTE] Example Point Distribution
> In the real world you have 3 Endurance, 1 Intuition, and -1 Vigor. You gain 3 Endurance Stat Points, 1 Intuition, and one negative Stat Point for Vigor.
> 
> You put 2 Points into **Strive** and 1 Point into **Hold** (from Endurance), 1 Point into **Discern** (from Intuition), and -1 Point into **Heave** (from Vigor).

Whenever you upgrade an XRRPG stat you may immediately gain a Stat Point and put it into a xenic plane Act of the same Stat.

If you ever do anything in the xenic plane that doesn't fit into any of these Acts just roll your Base Dice Pool.

Everybody gets the **Help** and **Willpower** Acts.

### Basic Act Examples

### Vigor

> When you **Surge** you may jump over a massive gulch, dash forward to escape a wall of flame, or lunge to grab an object falling into a whirlpool.
---
> When you **Heave** you may push a hulking stone into a gap to stop an incoming flood, lift a fallen log two times your height, or rip a door off its hinges.
---
> When you **Smash** you may take a hammer to a wall to break it down, throw a rock at a tree to snap it, or punch the ground to make a hollow.

### Endurance

> When you **Hold** you may stand strong in a torrential downpour, clasp the ground to resist a forceful gust of wind, or keep grasp of a friend's hand to prevent them falling into a pit.
---
> When you **Strive** you may continue walking far past the point of exhaustion, dig in the ground for hours and hours without rest, or carry a friend's weakened body to a safe location far away.
---
> When you **Resist** you may shrug off the effects of extreme cold or heat, ignore a devil's attempt to lead you astray, or keep conscious while affected by a deadly poison.

### Intuition

> When you **Poise** you may dodge an incoming rockslide, balance on a rope suspended high between two peaks, or catch a spirit whizzing through the air.
---
> When you **Discern** you may hear rushing water at the bottom of a chasm, sense a flash of red xeon in the dark of a cave, or see where a door from one section of the xenic plane to the next might be.
---
> When you **Commune** you may feel the grief of a group of elementals, sense the simmering anger of a teammate, or send a feeling of understanding and patience to a hostile devil.

### Wit

> When you **Examine** you may notice subtle tracks through the detritus of a forest floor, discern the origin of a magical item through the details of its design and manufacture, or analyze the bearing of a shade to determine its history and potential points of weakness and strength.
---
> When you **Influence** you may suggest a rational course of action to calm a fuming ally, redirect the despondency of a spirit to retribution towards its enemies, or convince an intelligent elemental that you have its best wishes in mind.
---
> When you **Divine** you may learn the location of the demon you're tracking down, read the dreams of an ally or enemy, or sense how close you are to a location in the physical realm.

Every Special Act is upgradable by spending Experience just like Basic Acts are, and each upgrades automatically when you level up a class in the real world.

If your XRRPG character is level 0, feel free to give them a Special Act that corresponds with the class that they *will* level up into when they hit level 1.

# Health

In the xenic plane killing is much more difficult than in the physical one, and requires an extremely powerful spirit or group of beings doing a ritual to **Sever** a spirit from their mortal body to *really* kill them.

Spirits can still deal damage to one another, however, and when a spirit reaches 0 Health they're Weakened and more susceptible to being **Bound**, **Banished**, or affected by another [[Rituals|ritual]].

Your Health equals your XRRPG HP stat divided by two (rounded down). 

## Dealing and Taking Damage

If you want to deal damage directly to a spirit you do a **Willpower** Act against them, where you roll your Base Dice Pool plus d8 equal to your strongest XRRPG stat vs their dice pool (which can be as high as 30d8 for the most powerful gods). 

Whoever wins the contest deals the other party damage equal to the difference in Belief.

> [!NOTE] Example **Willpower** Contest
> You find yourself in a 1v1 battle with El, the Primordial God of Magic.
> 
> You're level 7 with a Wit of 5 in the real world, and you have four friends **Helping** you, so you roll 12d8 (3d8 Base Dice Pool + 5d8 for your Wit of 5 + 4d8 from your friends). You roll *super* well, getting a total of 11 Belief.
> 
> El rolls a dice pool of 30d8, getting a total of 25 Belief. 25-11=14, so you take 14 damage.
> 
> You only have 7 HP (base 10+4 from the Armor Skin II Cybermod = 14 then divided by 2 for Xenic Plane Health). 
> 
> You're Weakened immediately. El then **Dissipates** your spirit, dissolving it into the xeon from whence it came.

As you can tell, some spirits will be much too strong for you to take alone or even as a group. Another piece of evidence to that: your Health max is 14, and spirits can have as much as 100 Health. To take down an especially strong spirit you might need to obtain a specific item that does increased damage against them or learn how to do a ritual to weaken them.

You can also attempt to deal damage by "physical" means, such as throwing a boulder or strangling your target with vines, but keep in mind that everything—including you and your teammates—is resistant to these types of attacks, and you'll only ever deal up to 3 or maybe 4 damage if successful.

How much damage a "physical" attack deals is always up to the discretion of the GM depending on how much Belief you rolled and what actions the target took to avoid your attack (and how successfully they rolled).

*It's almost always best to find a non-combat way to deal with any given spirit!* Most spirits will respond more to a diplomatic approach than a combative one, and often violence will cause much bigger problems than you started with. In addition, all but the weakest spirits have a *lot* more health than you, so keep that in mind as you attempt to deal with them.

Any injury taken from the environment or any other "physical" source usually deals from 1 to 3 damage.

## Hitting 0 HP and Healing

When you reach 0 Health you can only use your Base Dice Pool for any check! 

Healing is done with special objects or materials, the **Heal** ritual, or at certain holy sites automatically.

You can also heal to full by resting in a safe location.

# Intense Situations

Sometimes you'll get into situations where everyone will be trying to do something at once. In circumstances like this everybody, including any npcs or opponents, decide what Act they want to work at the start of the round and then act in an order determined by what kind of Act they chose to work.

If there's ever a tie between members of your party then the whole party decides who goes first, and if you ever tie with an opponent you both roll a d8 and whoever rolls higher goes first.

Each participant in the situation can only do one Act per Round! Spirits, on the other hand, can do anywhere from 1 to 10 Acts per Round.

## Round Order

1. Action Stage
	- All Acts except **Willpower**
	- You may take a penalty to do an Act quickly
		- Sacrifice any amount of d8, the Act gains Speed equal to that amount
		- Act with the highest Speed goes first
	- Can do a **Help** Act to give a teammate +1d8 on their next Act
	- For contesting Acts, the one with the higher Belief wins
2. **Willpower** Stage
	- Deal and take damage (see [[#Dealing and Taking Damage|above]])
3. Ritual Stage
	- Any Act done towards completing a ritual 
		- Rituals almost always require several Acts over more than one round
	- Can **Heal**, **Bind**, **Weaken**, and much more with [[Rituals]]

# Tips for GMs

To run a game in the xenic plane first of all you need to understand the realm you're delving into! Read through [[xr/Worldbuilding/Lore/The Xenic Plane]] to get a grasp of what it is, how it works, and what kinds of landscapes and beings you'll expect to see.

Beyond that, though, here are some tips to help you run things smoothly:

## The Experience

- Especially as you get deep into the xenic plane, *include your players* in the creation of how the xenic plane looks and feels! Remember that the psyches of the characters themselves shape the dreamworld they're entering.
- A massive part of your player's journey should be the *aesthetic experience* of traveling through the spirit world: describe otherworldly sights and sounds, strange touch sensations and pungent smells. 
- Let your story and mechanic choices flow from the lay of the land and the aesthetics you're describing. When in doubt, describe whatever comes to your mind first! You are crafting a dream world, after all.
- Don't be afraid of the mundane! A major part of making an experience feel cozy or warm and familiar is focusing on small actions, tiny objects, seemingly weak and inconsequential characters that end up meaning a lot to you personally. Think of a Miyazaki film and how much of the time is spent on small, precious details.
	- These sorts of otherwise tedious actions can be "sped up" in the grand scheme of play, and make sure not to *describe* them as tedious (unless they roll badly). Your players are in the flow!
	- For example, you're gathering a massive amount of an herb as a gift to a nature spirit. At first your characters hate the process, but as they roll well on **Strive** they start feeling the dirt beneath their feet, smelling loam as they pick these plants, and slowly they relax into their work. Pretty quickly they're done!
- Focus on what will feel meaningful to your characters: feel free to let the xenic plane mirror parts of their real lives. Lead them to places that feel and look familiar, have them talk to spirits who remind them of people they know in the real world.

## Acts

- Encourage your characters to try Acts that break the laws of physics and the logic of the physical world.
	- As long as it works for the story that you're shaping and doesn't make the road too easy for your characters, let them do fantastical things!
	- Show other beings acting out the far reaches of what's possible in the Xenic Plane to give the players ideas of what *their* characters are capable of.
	- On super high rolls feel free to warp time and space! This is the xenic plane, after all. 
- That said, feel free to say no! You can make an Act require way more Belief if they're trying to do something that will break the story or make the experience less fun for everyone.
	- The xeon itself is so tightly tied to the laws of physics and reality that *the xeon itself* can have a hard time believing you can break the rules; it fights against you when you try to ignore reality even though you're in a dreamworld.
	- You're in a dream world but there are *some* restrictions. Maybe a god has made a **Decree** to stop people from doing a certain thing or breaking a rule.

## Flow

- Consider letting your players stumble upon the instructions for a few important [[Rituals]] such as [[Rituals#Heal|Heal]], [[Rituals#Bind|Bind]], and [[Rituals#Barrier|Barrier]] to help them not be so helpless early on.
- Try to keep the story consistent: no changing the plot underneath their feet like happens in real dreams sometimes.
- Safe play is boring play: motivate the players to take risks, try things that will offend the spirits, or go for Acts they might fail at. 
	- Reassure them that failures and obstacles are just opportunities for character growth and ways to build more interesting situations!

## Test Them

Part of the point of the xenic plane is that it's a test of character, a stretching of belief and expectation, a way for players to delve into the minds of their characters. Make the experience meaningful by helping characters achieve hard things! Don't foil them, *test* them.

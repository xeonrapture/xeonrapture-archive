[[Spaceship Details]]

# Basics
You're piloting a vehicle! Could be a car, a hovership, a small pirate ship, etc.! 

If you're in massive ships that wouldn't take damage from a character's actions, ships that are enclosed and don't allow jumping between them, or ships that have large functionality of their own use [[Spaceship Rules]] instead!

# Stats
Your characters will be making all the necessary checks.

Rolling Stats:

| Main Stat  | Description                                                                |
| ---------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Agility    | How quick and dexterous your vehicle is                                    |
| Strength   | How strong your vehicle is: for ramming, towing, and moving through debris |
| Durability | How tough your vehicle is: for defense and resistance rolls                |
| Aim        | How responsive your vehicle's guns are                                     |

Secondary Stats:

| Stat               | Description                                                                                                           |
| ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| HP                 | How much health does the vehicle have? Usually anywhere from 50 to 300 depending on material and size of the vehicle. |
| Move Speed         | How far you can move in a turn                                                                                        |
| Passenger Capacity | How many passengers the vehicle can hold                                                                              |
| Cargo Capacity     | How much cargo the ship can hold; could be in pounds, tons, or whatever                                               |

# Stations and Actions
There are several stations your characters can be at and many things they can do at those stations. You must have a person at the station in order for your ship to take any of these actions!

You add only your stats to vehicle checks during combat! You may add proficiency dice and piloting pluses to any checks done outside of combat if it makes sense to do so (e.g. you're using a class action to operate the ship).

| Station        | Available Actions                                              | Char. Stats |
| -------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------- |
| Pilot's Seat   | Piloting, defending, ramming, vessel communication, front guns | Int, Wit    |
| Guns           | Shooting                                                       | Int, Wit    |
| Hooks, Tethers | Tethering other ships or objects                               | Vig, End    |

Pilots may use their stamina to make their own actions if they wish, but they must stay in the driver's seat to keep driving (obviously).

# Ship Sections
Your ship has a variety of sections that have different functionalities and have a certain amount of health before they go down. When taking damage, both the specific part of the ship and the ship as a whole will take damage. It takes one stamina to repair it 10 HP, and if the section is offline it will go online the turn after being repaired.

| Ship Section   | Function  | Result of Damage                          |
| -------------- | --------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Cockpit        | Piloting  | Loss of steering and thruster control     |
| Guns           | Shooting  | Loss of attack capabilities from that gun |
| Hooks, Tethers | Tethering | Loss of tethering capabilities            |

# Gameplay
You roll 2d10 on any check you do with the ship. All actions done on a vehicle use individual characters' stamina.

People must man the separate stations to use them, and if anybody has a bonus to actions with their specific station then they can take their bonus and add it to ship checks with that station.


> [!Example] Example Vehicle Check
> You're on a medium car in the gatling gun section on the top. You use the ship's Aim stat plus your own Int stat to do an attack roll (2d10 + car Aim stat + character Int stat).
> 
> The driver of the car you're attacking has a Defense DC lower than what you rolled. You beat the driver and do damage!

For combat, initiative is decided by a contested, flat d10 roll between each group's leader. Initiative is taken between ships, and the crew members decide together what the ship will do.

Here is the chart for the types of checks you can do while on a vehicle or ship:

| Type of Action | Check Math                              | Description                                 |
| -------------- | --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Vehicle Action | 2d10 + Vehicle Stat + Character Stat    | Checks done while using ship functionality. |
| Crew Action    | 2d10 + Character Stat + Proficiency Die | Checks done with character abilities.       |

## Attacking
There are different types of guns that do different things, and you may target different parts of a ship in order to damage functionality. See the ship sections for results of damage to certain areas.

You may also ram a vessel or vehicle to attack, which does a certain amount of dmg depending on your own vehicle. If you don't have the right shielding on the front of your vehicle, however, it will also deal half the dmg you deal back to your own vehicle.

Feel free to use your stamina on your own class actions! You may attack an enemy's vehicle to try to destroy it or disable a section, or you may attack an enemy directly. 

Get creative! Jump across into their vehicle, try to tip them over, kill the driver, etc.

## Defending
When defending against an attack you calculate the Defense DC using the Pilot Stat and the Vehicle Defense stat (Agi or Dura, you choose), and then your enemy will roll against your Defense DC to see whether they hit you or not. To calculate your Defense DC use this formula:

> [!Formula] 
> 11 + Pilot Stat (usually Int or Wit) + any Pilot plus + Vehicle Agi or Dura = Defense DC

For example, I'm the pilot of a ship and I use Int to pilot. I have 4 Int and the ship has 5 Agility, so my Defense DC would be 11 + 4 + 5 = 20.

> [!Warning] 
> If there is no Pilot in the driver's seat then the Defense DC = 11 + Vehicle Agi or Dura!

## Docking
To attempt to dock, do a piloting contest vs a piloting check of the enemy. If you win you successfully dock and board their vehicle!

## Reaching 0 HP
If your vehicle reaches 0 HP it then stalls and may crash if it's moving at any significant speed! 

In the event of a crash have everyone on the vehicle do a check to get to safety (if they have seat belts on or are using any other safety feature then give them a bonus or advantage to the check). 

If they fail the check then deal a significant amount of damage (maybe around 5d10, but it'll depend on the crash) and if they go unconscious subject them to one of the following Critical Injuries:
- [[Critical Injuries#Bleeding Out]] or [[Critical Injuries#Blunt Trauma]] if it isn't a serious crash or if they managed to mitigate most of the damage but still got reduced to 0 HP
- [[Critical Injuries#Bleeding Out FAST]] or [[Critical Injuries#Maimed]] if it was a super bad crash and they took the brunt of the damage!
- [[Critical Injuries#Demolished]] if the leftover damage after they got to reduced to 0 HP would've killed them over again.

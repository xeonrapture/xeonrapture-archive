# Intro

You're running the web! You start in your local node, which looks however you want it to look and is basically a home base.

Looking out of your node you see a net of twisting gray lines with the occasional flash of color, all connecting, pulsing, shifting information between nodes. When you enter a node you enter a whole new world, all depending on what node you enter.

Check here for more info on the [[Hikari Lore Drop#The Gray Web|gray web]].

# Stats

On the web, roll with a d20! And you only have a couple of stats:

| Stat       | Description                                                                     |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| HP         | Start with 10 + Vigor + 2x Endurance HP.                                        |
| Body       | Everything you do with your web body. Starts as the sum of Vigor and Endurance. |
| Mind       | Everything you do with your mind. Starts as the sum of Wit and Intuition.       |
| Defense DC | 11 + Body Stat + Armor Plus                                                     |

# Powers, Gear, and Leveling Up

As you traverse the web and defeat viruses and AIs you gain strength and gear. You can also buy weapons, armor, and mods, which are installed into the your webrunning cybermod

If you don't have anything installed, however, you start with the following gear.
- Simple Armor: +1 to Defense DCs
- Simple Melee Weapon: +1 to attack rolls and dmg, does a d10 of dmg, uses Body stat
- Simple Ranged Weapon: +1 to attack rolls and dmg, does a d8 of dmg, uses Mind stat
- 1 Healing Pod F: can consume to heal 1d10 + 2. You are only given this if you don't already have healing pods when starting a mission.

As you travel through the web, the enemies you defeat may become gear that you can equip for better stats (typically one piece of gear per enemy defeated). You can find Healing Pods anywhere.

| Enemy Type | Possible Drops |
| ---------- | -------------- |
| Virus      | Weapons        |
| Firewall   | Armor          |
| AI         | Modules        |

When you obtain gear while webrunning, this gear is installed on your webrunning cybermod and carries over into the next run.

## Status Effects

When trying to apply a status effect, such as warping, stunning, or pushing an enemy, that enemy will make one check that defends against both the damage and the status effect.

# Combat

Initiative is decided by a contested, flat d20 roll between each group's leader. Initiative is taken between teams. Your team decides who goes first within the team, and you can change the order every round.

Rounds take 10 in-game seconds.

## Attacking and Defending

You attack against a Defense DC. You add your body stat to the the attack roll and the attack damage, and the Defense DC uses the formula 11 + Body Stat + Armor Plus.

## Healing

Healing yourself does not take any actions, but healing someone else does!

# Fall Damage

There is no fall damage!

## Reaching 0 HP

You can heal your teammates with healing pods if they go down! That takes a turn though.

When you drop to 0 HP your cyberbody is dispersed and you come back as a cyberghost. You can't interact with the world as a cyberghost, but you can do Mind checks and add half your Body stat (min of 1) to any of your teammate's Body rolls as an action. When the group moves to another node your cyberbody rematerializes (but with half health)!

If your whole team goes down you return to your physical bodies and you drop anything you gained that run. If you go down inside a secure or locked system you'll have to escape as cyberghosts with the whole system trying to catch you!

# Hacking

When you hack, you roll your mind stat each round against a decaying DR. Each round that your hacking there's a chance that something will happen, such as a defense response, the hacker taking damage, or, at the most extreme, a anti-hacking virus trying to find out your location in the physical world. You won't know the possible responses until you begin hacking.

Also, too many failures and whatever you're trying to hack will lock down, possibly sending reinforcements, dealing damage to the would-be hacker, or dealing out some other gnarly consequence.

> [!example]
> You begin hacking a cache of money. The decaying DR is 40, looks like this'll be a toughie. 
> 
> You roll a 15 to start, nice, the DR goes down to 25. The GM rolls on a table, oh looks like they found you! Your team has to fight off a couple of firewall defense programs.
> 
> You roll a 5, damn. The DR goes down to 20, the GM rolls again. Surprise! They employ counter-hacking viruses that your team has to beat, and is now more overwhelmed than ever.
> 
> You roll again, a 14, so close! DR is down to 6, but the GM rolls and says they're now trying to figure out where your physical bodies are. Better hurry!
> 
> You roll one more time, and you get a 7! Nice, you're in. You grab all the cash, and just in time, too; if you had failed they would've found out your real-life location. You destroy the defense programs and viruses, collect their loot, and are on your way.

# Traversing the Web

You get onto the web by hooking your mind up to a terminal (usually through a cyberport installed in your head), which then connects either to a nexus or directly to a node on the web. From the nexus or the node you can then travel to another node or nexus, or enter the space between them. Nexuses are hubs that have a bunch of nodes connected to them.

Often to get to the node you want you'll have to travel through other nodes which can be full of viruses, AIs, firewall defenses, traps, puzzles, and other threats and obstacles. Nodes are shaped by whoever created them or maintains them, and often have environments similar to that of real life places.

Some nodes have been abandoned! These often still have caches of credits, knowledge, or items in them, and are sometimes still protected, sometimes not.

## Choosing a Route

When traversing the web you'll usually be presented with several different node routes you can take, each with their own particular risks and possible rewards.

Each node on a possible route will have certain characteristics:
- Danger Level: nodes are ranked in danger from I to X
- Node Contents: each node will reveal what type of vibe each node has
	- Puzzle
	- Trap
	- Combat
	- ???
- Loot Type: nodes might have special loot within, either hidden, guarded, or locked behind a puzzle or trap
	- Credits
	- Gear Drop
	- Mod Box
	- Essence Gem
	- Lore Codex
	- Hidden Loot

There should be enough loot dropped for everybody in the party, whether it be credits, gear, mods, or essence.

## Returning to Your Home Node

At any time after clearing a node of danger your party may return right back to your home node, even if you're in a secure or locked system! You'll have to return to the entrance to the node to leave if you haven't cleared the node.

Just remember that if you are in a secure or locked system and you decide to leave you'll have to go through a node route again if you return, which will have repopulated its threats or even changed entirely.

## Recording Node Addresses

When you get where you're wanting to go or just want to return to a location later you can record the address of the node and return to it whenever you'd like! Unless you've entered a secure system, in which case you can record the address of a node just outside the system but can't record locations inside the system.

## Places on the Web

| Depth      | Controlled By | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| ---------- | ------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Normal Web | Govts, Corps  | Stores all the normal data, sometimes protected sometimes not. Consists of nodes that are connected to one central nexus that acts as a search engine.                                                                    |
| Deep Web   | Govts, Corps  | More protected, every database has got a firewall and viruses and/or AIs protecting it. Government and mob databases. Need to find your way in or make your own connection.                                               |
| Dark Web   | No one, Mobs  | Even deeper, everyone's anonymous and you need a special device to get into it. More viruses, more protection, more illicit behavior. Gotta be armed to the teeth.                                                        |
| Old Web    | No one        | The leftovers of anything anyone in the area of the supercluster has put on the web or encoded in a way that the web can connect to. Literally anything could be on here, and it's even more dangerous than the dark web. |

## Opportunities on the Web

- Meet randos, AIs
- Find old troves of knowledge, or caches hidden away by governments, corporations, or mobs
- Find stashes of money! Many people hide credits on the web

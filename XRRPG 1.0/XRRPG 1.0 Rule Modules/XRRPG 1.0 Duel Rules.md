# General Rules

DUELS. Think cowboy movies! Only the quickest draw survives.

The GM will tell you if a specific encounter is a duel or not! Though if the GM *doesn't* say that it's a duel and you think that it might be a good opportunity for one then feel free to ask the GM if the encounter can be a duel!

There are two types of Duels: 1v1 and Group Duels. 1v1 is simple: you do contested attack rolls and deal damage until one character drops. See below for the rules on Group Duels.

> [!1v1 Rules]
> Here are the rules for 1v1s:
> 1. Both people do an attack roll.
> 2. Winner hits first! The attack autohits and uses your Stamina Max no matter how much stamina you have stored up.
> 3. If the other duelist is still standing, they attack back using their Stamina Max.
> 4. If both duelists are still up then you reset back to step 1.
> 5. If someone dies during the duel then they are subject to the [[Critical Injuries#Bleeding Out FAST|Bleeding Out FAST]] Critical Injury!

More Rules:
- Ties means both people deal damage at the same time. 
- If you'd like to amp up the intensity you may have the attack autocrit and/or be subject to the [[Critical Injuries#Demolished|Demolished]] Critical Injury when you die during a duel.

# Group Duels

For Group Duels ideally the GM will pit you against a group that has the same amount of enemies as there are characters in your party, and each player picks an individual enemy that they get to duel with. 

From there you just run multiple 1v1 duels. Tell the GM what you rolled and then the GM will tell you who won and who takes damage.

At the end of the first round the GM will size up who's still standing. If it's still a hostile situation, the GM should have each player still standing secretly communicate to them what they want to do: surrender or attack (or whatever else they might want to do).

If they decide to attack they need to roll both an attack roll and damage roll in secret and tell the GM who they're attacking and what they rolled. They can even decide to attack their own teammates (though this, along with most PvP, is discouraged if playing a long-running campaign where inter-party trust is important).

Then the GM figures it all out and tells the remaining characters what happens! Perfect for parties that are not necessarily loyal to the other members (such as at the end of a heist, where the survivor will take home all the spoils).

[[_XRRPG MASTERSHEET]]

So normally when you go down you're just unconscious with no threat of death. BUT if an attack roll beats you by over 10 or if you take over 20 dmg in one hit then you take a critical injury, which means that there IS a chance you'll die when you go down.

Whenever you take a critical injury that means that whatever hit you will probably leave a scar of some sort! Think about what kind of scar your character would have, physical or mental, and what it would look like and how you could integrate it into your character's conception about themselves.

Do critical injury checks at the top of your turn! When you come back after going down you retain half of your stamina from before. If you go down and are revived you still retain your previous critical injury checks from before (so if you go down again you still retain your successes or failures).

If you roll your critical injury and are stable that clears the critical injury from you! So if you get healed and go down again then you don't have that critical injury to deal with.

Critical injuries are completely resolved when you heal to full or when you do a Shallow or Deep Rest.

# Normal
## Bleeding Out
Roll a d10 each turn:
- Every roll 1-5 counts as a failure and every roll 6-10 counts as a success. 
- Three failures = you die, and three successes = you're stable!

## Blunt Trauma
Roll 3d6 every turn: 
- 1s count as failures and 6s count as successes! 
- Three failures = you die, and three successes = you're stable.

# More Intense
## Withering
Have to crit on a d8.

Every turn you roll the number you die at creeps up, starting at 1, then moving to 2, then to 3, etc. 

If you ever roll at or below the death number then you die.
## Bleeding Out FAST
Roll a d10 each turn: 
- Every roll 1-5 counts as a failure and every roll 6-10 counts as a success. 
- TWO failures = you die, and three successes = you're stable!

## VERY Blunt Trauma
Roll 3d6 every turn: 
- 1s count as failures and 6s count as successes! 
- TWO failures = you die, and three successes = you're stable.

## Necrosis
Have to crit on a d8, and if that doesn't work you try on a d10 next turn, then on a d12 the turn after that, and finally a d20.

If you fail on a d20 you die.
## Maimed
Use this for a deep, deep wound or getting crushed by something massive.

Roll a 1d6. If you don't get surgical medical attention within 1d4 hours the results are permanent (and for a 1 you're dead). 
	- 6=right leg maimed
	- 5=left leg maimed
	- 4=right arm maimed
	- 3=left arm maimed
	- 2=spine broken
	- 1=head broken

## Disease and Poison
### Deterioration
You are immediately stable, but incapacitated. Do a 1d4 roll per hour, and on 3 1s you die. You must find a cure or other intense medical attention!
### Intense Disease
Roll a 1d4, on a 1 you instantly die, otherwise you're fine.
### Brutal Poison
You remain conscious but in immense pain. You must make a defense End check against the poison or disease itself every 10 minutes.

The disease or poison has a +4 Int modifier to the attack and deals as many d8s of dmg as you have levels. As soon as the damage wraps around your health again, you die.

You must find a cure, have someone cure you, or get the poison out somehow.

# Death Blows
These are for wraparound dmg, when you get knocked unconscious and the leftover dmg would destroy entirety of your HP again.

## Demolished
For physical sources of dmg.

Roll 1d20. 
- On a 20 you suffer the blunt trauma critical injury above. 
- On a 19 you survive but permanently maimed (roll on maim table). 
- On 2-18 you instantly die. 
- On a 1 you instantly die *and* your spirit anchor is also destroyed. 
## Obliterated
For xenic sources of dmg.

Roll 1d10
- On a 10 you suffer the blunt trauma critical injury above. 
- On 2-9 you are instantly disintegrated. 
- On a 1 you are instantly disintegrated *and* your spirit anchor is also destroyed.
## Disintegrated
For insane amounts of dmg, gods deciding to unmake you, that sort of stuff.

Roll a 1d100.
- On a 100 you suffer the Maimed critical injury above. 
- On all other numbers you and your spirit anchor are completely destroyed.

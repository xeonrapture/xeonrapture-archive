# Basic Character Creation
Click [[XRRPG Character Creation Template#Basic Stats|here]] for a Character Creation Template you can use!

- [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Chroma|Chroma]] and [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Stats|Stats]]
	- Choose a chroma: Red, Blue, Green, or Grey. Each one has a corresponding stat.
		- Red: Vigor
		- Blue: Endurance
		- Green: Initiative
		- Grey: Wit
	- Take +2 to your chroma stat or +3 to your chroma stat and a -1 to another stat
	- You can also take a [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Chroma|clear chroma]] if you'd like, but that means you can't take the adept class.
	- As you [[Leveling Up|level up]] you upgrade your stats.
- Species
	- Check out the [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Species|Species]] and pick one of them!
	- Each species has visual characteristics and a common chroma that you may take if you like.
- [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Proficiency Die|Proficiency Die]]
	- At Level 1 you have a d4 Proficiency Die, which gets better as you [[Leveling Up|level up]].
	- You can add this die to checks you do using class abilities as well as attacks and some checks to resist status effects.
- Stamina
	- At Level 1:
		- You gain 2 Stamina per Turn.
		- Your Stamina Max is 4 (amount of stamina you can have stored up at any given time).
	- You get more stamina per turn as you [[Leveling Up#Stamina Leveling|level up]].
- [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Classes|Classes]]
	- Pick a class! Classes grant magical abilities, technological skills, or superpowers.
		- [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#_Adepts|Adepts]] have superpowers that are tied to their chroma.
		- [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Mages|Mages]] make pacts with gods, demons and devils that grant them support abilities, healing powers, and damage.
		- [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Magicians|Magicians]] learn the a magical language that control the xeon and grant them flexible power and utility.
		- [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#_Technologists|Technologists]] can create gadgets and weapons that give them all sorts of skills and abilities.
	- Click [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Class Reference Chart|here]] for a class reference chart with more details.
- [[Leveling Up#Gaining Health|Health]]
	- Start with 10 + 1d4 HP for Level 0.
	- For Level 1, take the Health Die for the class that you pick, roll it, and add that + your Endurance Stat to your Max HP.
		- If you don't like what you roll you may either reroll your Health Die and take the new roll or add your Health Die divided by two to your Max HP.
	- For each level after 1 you'll roll the Health Die again and add that + your End Stat to your Max HP.
- Skills
	- At Level 1 you start with 2 skills which may be General or Specific.
		- General Skills get a +2
			- Some examples of General Skills are stealth, insight, persuasion, and investigation.
		- Specific Skills get a +3
			- Some examples of Specific Skills are slinging, intimidation, nature, history, cooking, and plumbing.
	- Look at the [[Skill List]] for ideas, but you can pick anything you want! 
		- A good rule of thumb is if it seems like a skill would affect a wide range of checks or would be commonly used in a normal campaign then it's probably a General Skill. Stealth only gets a +2 because it's a pretty common skill check to use.
	- Check [[Leveling Up]] for at which levels you upgrade skills and get new skills.

# [[Leveling Up]]
- Milestone Leveling: You level up when your GM decides you do! There is no XP.
- Class Levels: You take a level in any class you'd like each time you level up! Most classes have a max of 6 levels, and some have additional levels beyond that.
- Health: Every level you roll your class's Health Die and add what you roll + Endurance stat to your Max HP.
	- If you don't like what you roll you may either reroll your Health Die and take the new roll or add your Health Die divided by two to your Max HP.
- Skill and Stat Upgrades: You gain more skills and stat plusses as you level.
- Total Character Level Max is 18.

# Basic Rules
Check [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules]] for more detailed rules.

- [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Adventuring|Adventuring]]
	- Basic Roll: 2d10 + Stat + ([[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Skills|Skill Plus]], if you're using a skill) + ([[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Proficiency Die|Proficiency Die]], if you're using class powers or attacking)
	- Stats: There are 4 [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Stats|stats]], each with slightly-overlapping aspects.
		- Vigor: Movement, burst power, force of will
		- Endurance: Toughness, endurance power, resilience, endurance
		- Intuition: Dexterity, instinct, creativity, perception, charisma
		- Wit: Learning, innovation, recall, synthesizing data
- [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Stamina|Stamina]]
	- Stamina Basics: You get a pool of stamina that you may use on attacks and class actions.
	- Resting: You may take a [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Resting|Shallow or Deep Rest]] to heal yourself and clear your [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Exhaustion|Total Stamina Spent]].
- [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Classes|Classes]]
	- Class Damage Dice: Each class has a list of basic damage dice they use when they do melee attacks, ranged attacks, and AOE attacks.
	- Class Abilities: Each class gets to choose certain abilities, and each class has their own set of vibes and rules.
- [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Combat|Combat]]
	- Starting
		- Initiative: Team leader rolls a d10 and whoever's team rolls higher goes first.
		- Team Turns: Your whole team takes their turns together, spending stamina to do actions and deal damage in whatever order you want!
	- Combat Stamina
		- Starting Stamina: You start combat with half of your [[Leveling Up#Stamina Leveling|Stamina Max]], and may use as many as you want on your turn.
		- Stamina Per Turn: You get a certain amount of stamina back per turn at the end of the turn (see [[Leveling Up]] for details).
	- Attacking
		- Attack Roll: Attacks are done by doing a 2d10 attack roll against a defender's Defense DC (11 + Stat + Defense Bonuses)
		- Damage = (damage die + stat) per stamina
		- Damage Dice: The damage die you use on an attack is determined by your class damage dice (listed at the top of a class sheet) or weapon damage dice if you're using a weapon.
		- Attack Stamina: You may spend any number of stamina on an attack, which makes you roll more dice and deal more damage if you hit on that attack!
	- Defending
		- Basic DDC: You defend with Defense DC equal to 11 + Stat you're using to defend + any Defense Bonuses you have, including armor and a shield.
		- DDC Choice: You may usually defend using any stat you wish!
		- Status Effects: You roll 2d10 + Stat (usually of your choice) + (Proficiency Die if you're using your chroma stat) to resist against taking a Status Effect.

## Other Rules
- Adventuring
	- Load Limit: You can only have up to 2 large weapons or implements on your person at one time, and only 5 weapons total. More can be stored in a pack or storage device.
	- Fall Damage: You take a d10 of dmg for each 10 ft of height you fall down
	- Pushing the Roll: If you want to retry a check you may reroll it and take a level of Spirit Decay. The effects of Spirit Decay come into effect after you finish the roll, check [[Spirit Decay]] for more details.
	- Gaining More Skills: You may start a [[Gaining New Skills and Decaying DRs|Decaying DR]] to gain a new skill!
- Stamina
	- Wear: You take [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Wear|wear]] when you push your body to its limits, including when you don't sleep regularly, you don't eat or drink water, or you're exposed to extreme elements. 
	- Overcharging: Most abilities that cost stamina can be [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Overcharging|overcharged]], meaning that you can spend more stamina on them and they'll get powerful. As you level up your [[Leveling Up|overcharge limit]] will go up, meaning you will be able to spend more stamina on any given ability.
	- Supercharging: Any ability can also be [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Supercharging!|supercharged]], which enables you to spend stamina you don't have but gives you a level of [[Spirit Decay]].
	- Exhaustion Limit: When you spend lots of stamina without resting you might hit your [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Exhaustion|exhaustion limit.]]
- Combat
	- Attack Advantage/Disadvantage: Attacking somebody who is using a certain stat to defend may make you attack with [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Attack Advantage/Disadvantage|disadvantage or advantage]]!
	- [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Grappling|Grappling Rules]]
	- Elite Enemies: Certain enemies are considered [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Fighting Elite Enemies and Party Tier|Elites]] and will incur a penalty on your checks against them unless you work together and your party is of a certain level!
	- Damage Types: Certain defenses are more effective against certain [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Damage Types|types of damage]]! Certain enemies are vulnerable or resistant to certain types of damage.
	- Critical Injuries and Damage: When you go unconscious there's only a threat of your character dying if you've [[XRRPG 1.0 Core Rules#Critical Injuries and Dying|hit certain conditions]].

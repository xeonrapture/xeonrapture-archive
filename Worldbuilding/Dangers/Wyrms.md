# Description

There are two types of wyrms: wyverns and lindwyrms.

Wyrms are everywhere, and not all of them are genetically similar: wyrms are kinda like crabs, where the pattern is just so successful that it shows up everywhere across the supercluster in different forms.

All wyrms are very resistant against xenic effects (including onyx and obsidian) as well as very tough, scaly skin (or metal skin if they're non-bio) so they're really hard to kill.

Young of either type are called smallwyrms, and it's almost impossible to tell whether a smallwyrm is going to grow into a lindwyrm or a wyvern because they don't have legs or wings.

## Wyverns

Wyverns are massive winged beasts, similar to dragons but with only two legs and their wings have claws on the ends of their wings that they use as front legs when they need to. They're fairly smart but are still beasts at the end of the day; they can go on rampages and are very territorial.

Most wyrms have some sort of breath weapon such as a flame or ice blast, but some just use their claws and extremely sharp teeth.

There are several different sub-species of wyverns, some that are entirely biological with heavy armor or light and agile, and others that are completely xenic, with bodies made of stone and metal and getting their energy from eating minerals and other xenic life.

Wyverns are social creatures and almost always subsist in a community, whether it be big or small.

## Lindwyrms

Lindwyrms are wyverns but with two arms instead of wings and no hind legs. They have their own unique type of magic that enables them to meld through stone and create tunnels, paralyze their prey, see the whole electromagnetic spectrum and into the xenic plane, and speak with spirits (though the powers can vary depending on where they evolved).

Lindwyrms are quite a bit smarter than wyverns but not *quite* as smart as mortals, hitting maybe great-ape level intelligence. They're typically solitary but sometimes move and live in groups.

## Wyvernriders

The Blackfyre House on Ydilik figured out how to gain enough trust with a wyvern to the point where you can get on one's back and ride it! These people get access to a massive amount of power and freedom as long as they respect their wyrmsteeds.

Only people with clear chromas can become wyvernriders.

## Goal

Wyrms are beasts, and so have the goals that all beasts have: survive, gain security, hold territory so they can have dependable sources of food and water, reproduce, raise their young.

Wyverns can be trained (more or less), and a trained wyvern has a similar set of goals as well as the goals of their masters.

# Locations

The place with the most wyrms is Ydilik (though they used to exist in Valda until they were killed off), where they are regarded from anything to pests (in the Kingdom of Eyyendal), holy beings (in some drokk cities and many places on the Kai continent), or even partners (the wyvernriders of House Blackfyre).

There are a bunch of wyverns that exist in space (called, fittingly, space wyverns), mostly in the Kotharak Field but in other places too. These wyverns are non-bio and subsist on xenic substances, metals, minerals, and the xeon itself.

It's not really a known fact because this place is very sequestered off from the rest of the mortals, but Aëanor has maybe even more wyrms than Ydilik does.

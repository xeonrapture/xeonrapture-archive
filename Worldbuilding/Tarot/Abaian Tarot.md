# Intro

The Abaian Tarot has a card for each Abaian god, including the Archons and Abai Himself. In contrast to the Eosian Tarot, each card in the Abaian Tarot stands on its own, asking you questions, allowing you to hold up its values to yourself and see what the card draws out of you.

The gods are direct, representing their domain and advocating for it, but the gods are also subtle, understanding that the boundaries of their domains are more vague than one might expect, like the banks of a river: ever-changing, flooding and expanding its bounds, running low in the dry season.

Let your mind go where the card takes you; the questions below are meant as guides, not as prescriptive rules.

# Ways to Read

Every card reader will have their own style; some will go entirely by their intuition, others will be more analytical. Some take readings as a prediction of the future, others take them as advice and direction.

Any reading can be done with a certain conflict, trial, or life situation in mind. The reader may use their own psychic or emotion-sensing talents to make their readings even more accurate.

Some of the most powerful readers are seers who have the power to see threads of the future, or have become so sensitive to the xenic plane that they can see what aura follows the one they're trying to read.

The gods can also grant their own help to a reading, giving insight that directs the reading a certain way. There's a tradition among clerics of giving readings with their own patron god in mind, which can change the reading significantly.

# Readings

## One-Card Reading

Pick a card to read in relation to any given aspect of your life, or towards your direction as a whole. Pick a card to designate a negative: something that you *shouldn't* be focusing on, or should be taken as a field of caution. Pick a card to help solve a specific problem.

## Multi-Card Readings

### Two-Card

One card for your past, one for your future. One for the positive, one for the negative, in opposition or contrast. One for yourself, one for a friend, lover, or relative.

One for the sky and one for the ground. One for the self and one for the other. One for action and one for protection.

### Three-Card

Past, present, and future. Situation, challenge, and solution. Sun, moon, star.

### Four-Card

The most common four-card reading is a card for red, blue, green, and gray. The red is the Action, what values should be directing the way you move; blue is the Protection, how you protect yourself and the ones around you; green is Instinct, something to use to plumb your own unconscious; and gray is Intellect, something to keep in mind, to ponder, to use as a guide for the future.

# Tarot

Abai, Father God: Who do you care for, and who cares for you? Who can you take care of, and who can help take care of you? In what ways can you better understand yourself and the people around you in order for you to serve and live well? Try to see the whole of mortal experience, all the things that make us who we are, and see how you fit into the big picture.

## Archons

[[4 Beings/1 Gods/1 Light Gods/3 Abaian/1 Archons/Ai|Ai]], Goddess of Wisdom: Are you understanding and gentle with yourself and others? Are you patient with the process and pace of your life? Do you seek to understand before judging?

[[4 Beings/1 Gods/1 Light Gods/3 Abaian/1 Archons/Torrena|Torrena]], Goddess of Survival: What can you do to make yourself more physically secure? How can you help the people around you survive and thrive? How can you prepare for rough times in the future?
[[4 Beings/1 Gods/1 Light Gods/3 Abaian/1 Archons/Vaiana|Vaiana]], Goddess of Change: How are you changing? How is the world around you changing? [[4 Beings/1 Gods/1 Light Gods/3 Abaian/1 Archons/Exon|Exon]], God of Progress: Are you moving forward in your life? Are you helping the organizations you are a part of move forward? Are you progressing at the right pace for you?

[[4 Beings/1 Gods/1 Light Gods/3 Abaian/1 Archons/Rtolka|Rtolka]], God of Knowledge: What do you know that others could benefit from? How can you better organize your own knowledge and the info around you? Are you being truthful with yourself and with those around you?
[[4 Beings/1 Gods/1 Light Gods/3 Abaian/1 Archons/Gatrathki|Gatrathki]], God of the One: You are unique: are you respecting that uniqueness? Are you willing to stand up for what you believe in? How can you include the people around you who feel alone and help them feel seen?
[[4 Beings/1 Gods/1 Light Gods/3 Abaian/1 Archons/Osadona|Osadona]], Goddess of the Many: You are situated in a community: how are you supporting it? ## Lesser Pantheon

### Ai

[[Anathia]], Goddess of Language: Is your language constructive? Are you using your words to reveal truth or conceal? In what ways do you and the people around you use language to build or destroy?
[[Eonath]], Goddess of Healing: Do you need to take some time to heal? Do you need to give your mind and body time to rest? How can you help the people around you heal?
[[Uio]], Goddess of Oracles: What does your future hold? How has your past affected your present? How can you better prepare for the future and honor your past?

### Toko

[[Kavak]], God of Protection: Are there people around you that need protection? How can you fight for them? How can you make the world a safer place?
[[Kol]], God of Leadership: In what ways can you lead the ones around you? How can you prepare to lead better? If you don't feel comfortable leading, are you following the right people?

### Torrena

[[Goyano]], God of Food and Drink: Are you healthy? If not, how can you get healthy? Do you enjoy your food and drink? What can you do to enjoy it more and share what you have with others?
[[Harkanath]], Goddex of the Hunt: Are you keeping your body strong and healthy enough to help others and go after your goals? Are you willing to keep working when your goals seem far away?
[[Ophai]], Goddess of the Harvest: What seeds are you planting that you will reap later? How are you working, and in what kinds of ways are you changing from your work? How can you build and save for a better future?
[[Vaathi]], God of Travel: Are you showing grace and understanding for the people around you as you travel through life? What kinds of experiences do you need to have in order to become the person you want to be? Where should you go to be in the right place at the right time?

### Vaiana

[[4 Beings/1 Gods/1 Light Gods/3 Abaian/1 Archons/2 Lesser/4 Vaiana/Boffm|Boffm]], God of Music: What themes do you hear playing through your life? What threads do you see tying it all together? How have those themes and your experiences changed you? How can you share the things you've learned with others?
[[Coreli]], Goddex of Art: Try to see the totality of your life: what moods color your life right now? How have your past experiences shaped what your life looks like now? Does your life need balancing? Are the wrong things in frame?
[[Estrid]], Goddess of Beauty: What beauty can you see in the world around you? How can you build on that beauty? How can you highlight and share that beauty for people around you?
[[Frode]], God of Stories: What stories do you tell yourself? What stories do you believe? Are they true stories? How can you better understand the narrative of your life? Are stories that your society, your nation, and your community tell you worthwhile and true?
[[Nytr]], Goddess of Dance: Are you flexible in your plans? Do you lose momentum when confronted by struggles? Do you take care of your body in a way that will keep you prepared for the future? 
[[Otho]], God of Theater: What masks do you wear, what characters do you put on, and for what reasons? How can you make your personalities more true to yourself and what you believe in? 

### Exon

[[Dovax]], Goddess of Science: How well do you understand the way that the world works? What things can you learn that will help you move through life with more grace and clearer vision? Are you ignoring things about how the world actually works in order to preserve your own worldview?
[[Exidath]], God of Technology: How are you using technology? Do you use technology to help yourself and the people around you? Or are you focusing on it more than the people it should be helping? How much of your time do you spend on new technologies?
[[Kaiatha]], Goddess of Industry: How hard do you work? Do you work at the right pace for you? Does your work build or destroy?

### Pbosh

[[Aisa]], Goddess of Romantic and Sexual Love: If you have romantic and sexual love in your life, are you taking care of it? Are you putting the time into it in order to keep it flourishing? If you don't, are you taking care of your personal emotional life like you would that of a significant other? Are you gentle with yourself and others around you?
[[Bai]], Goddess of Celebration: What do you have in your life that you can celebrate? What can you do to celebrate with the people that you care about? Let yourself be proud of what you deserve to be proud of.
[[Gaia]], Goddess of Courage: Do you feel comfortable standing up for what you believe in? Do you have the confidence and strength to do the hard things that need doing? If not, start small: do hard things consistently and your courage will grow.
[[Yoi]], God of Humor: Do you make space for humor in your life? Can you loosen up enough to laugh at yourself and others in healthy ways?

### Rtolka

[[Holtana]], Goddess of History: How are you honoring your past? Do you understand your personal past and the past of your society? How can you learn from history?
[[Natoh]], God of Numbers: How can you understand more about the world? What underlying rules of how the world works could help you be more effective?
[[Noia]], Goddess of Teaching and Learning: How can you share your experiences and knowledge with the people around you? What things do you know that could help others around you? What are subjects that you could explore that would enrich your life and make you more whole?
[[Riia]], God of Secrets: What hidden truths do you hold dear? What are questions about the world that you would like to explore further? Often the smaller truths, the hidden details are the most consequential.
[[Vioth]], Goddess of Philosophy: Do you understand yourself? Do you understand why you believe what you believe? Do you understand where other people are coming from and how they think?

### Osadona

[[Monatha]], Goddess of Familial Love: Are you building family around you, whether biological or found? Are you helping and serving the family that you have? What can you learn from your family?
[[Olopan]], God of Play: Are you including fun into your life? Are you playing in constructive ways, or are you wasting some time?

## Other

Dodat, God of the Dead: How can you honor your ancestors? Are you living a life that you can be proud of? Death comes for us all, and all we can do is make this life as good as we can for ourselves, the people around us, and future generations.
The Argent, Chosen One: How can you become the person that you want to be, and the kind of person that can serve others? What do you need to learn, what skills do you need to practice? What is holding you back?

# Intro

The Eosian Tarot is a journey, a way to reflect on where you are, where you've come, and where you're going. The most common way to do an Eosian Tarot reading is to pull a card for the Past, another for the Present, and a last for the Future, but each reader will have their own methods.

Each Eosian Tarot deck includes ten cards of each color—red, blue, green, and gray—and each of the sixteen Eosian God cards below.

# Color Card Journey

Below are the general details on what each card in the journey means; each color's journey will be different, as each color deals with different values.

1. Mote: You start with a small, colored mote in your hands. It fills you with a feeling: a desire to work, a feeling of passion, a glimmer of creativity, or the seed of an idea. You place the mote inside of you, where it begins to grow.
2. Hands: You move forward, reaching out, starting to create and give of your newfound feeling.
3. River: You begin to walk beside a river which leads you and shows you the way. The journey is slow, but you keep moving.
4. Waterfall: You come up to a waterfall that shows you the pitfalls of your new journey and the mote that is growing inside of you.
5. Eye: As you continue forward, your eyes have been opened and you see the good and bad around you; you witness how your feeling affects you and others around you.
6. Sea: You reach a massive sea who teaches you the full depths of your new feeling. It shows you gratitude for what you've been given and teaches you new ways to give and support others. It helps you accept what you have and what you don't.
7. Cloud: You look up and see the clouds, their beauty and uniqueness, and learn from how they change and flow.
8. Mountain: You begin to climb a mountain; the journey is very difficult and you feel like giving up but you continue onward.
9. Dragon: You reach the top of the mountain and are greeted by a dragon. You mount the dragon who begins to fly. You are filled with great power and responsibility.
10. Planet: The dragon flies to a (Red: star, Blue: barren planet, Green: lush planet, Gray: mercury planet) and you contemplate its majesty and power and the wholeness of its being. You see how the planet fits into the system that it's been placed into. You begin to understand more deeply what this mote inside you actually means. You complete a cycle.

# Color Cards

## Red
1. Mote: You may feel like your passion is too weak for you to rely on, too small for it to motivate you to do anything. Start with what you have! Let the enthusiasm you have motivate you to act, however small the thing it pushes you to do is. Make a habit of letting your pure passions motivate you to action and pretty soon you'll find yourself filled with strength. These motes of passion may be the first steps on a long journey.
2. Hands: Take what passion and ambition you have and create with it! Give and share your enthusiasm and energy with others. Set goals small enough that you feel like you can reach them but big enough that you'll feel great when you complete them, and then work! When you complete a goal, share your achievement with others! Accept and support the passion of others.
3. River: Your confidence may take a while to develop; this is normal. As you act on your enthusiasm you'll begin to trust it to lead you in good directions, and as you grow and change your passion will grow with you. Don't be afraid of slowing down or speeding up if your current pace feels wrong! Respect your emotions and honor what they tell you.
4. Waterfall: Passion and ambition can blind you and lead you into traps of your own creation! Be extra cautious of staying dead-set on one solution over all others. Your enthusiasm can cloy if directed at the wrong target; stay moving, but make sure that you don't rush in.
5. Eye: Both the good and the bad in this world generate a lot of passion: our deepest and most powerful feelings are tied to both the worst and the best things we've experienced. Don't reject either the good or the bad! Understand them both and how they motivate your emotions. Witness your emotions and their origins, no matter how uncomfortable it might make you.
6. Sea: Recognize that whatever energy and passion you have is a gift! Be grateful for the well of emotion that you've been given. Accept that if you're feeling low that you may just need time and healing in order to fill your well of passion back up. Accept that others may be feeling low, understand their needs, and help them with your own passion and energy. Be gentle with yourself and others.
7. Cloud: Everybody's energy and enthusiasm is unique, both in direction and in quality. Some people have loud, exuberant passion, and others' energy is more quiet. Some people work in spurts, others more slowly and doggedly. Accept that your passion and ambition is what it is! There is beauty in your difference, purity in the things you care about. Lean into your existing pure passions!
8. Mountain: Sometimes your enthusiasm will wane and you'll begin to question whether your energy has been misguided from the beginning. Take stock of these doubts and honestly evaluate where you are and why you're feeling what you are. Redirect your passion if you feel you're going down the wrong path, but if you come away feeling that you're in the right place, keep going strong! Find things to do that will help you rekindle your excitement for whatever you're working towards. You can do it!
9. Dragon: Your passion and ambition, whatever it is and however strong it may feel to you, grants you power. Make sure to take responsibility for your emotions and your ambition! Give of your enthusiasm and make sure it's infused with kindness and patience. Talk to others of your dreams and let them share their passions with you. Find others who share your passions and support them in their journeys.
10. Planet: As you start, continue, and complete cycles of passion and enthusiasm, realize that passion is just one part of what makes a person whole: balance it with patience and knowledge. Let passion infuse your life and leaven your interactions with others. Make sure your passion is directed at the right goals and is working through pure means. Your energy may be quiet or loud, but let it room to grow and it will influence everything you do!

## Blue
1. Mote: You might not have much skill, or you might be low on funds or physical means. This is a starting point for you! Work as hard as you can to learn and leverage what you have in order to create stability for yourself and others.
2. Hands: As much as you can, create and reach out with your productivity and your skill. If you have physical means to share, share it, and if you don't, reach out and ask. Sometimes asking for help can connect you with others as much as helping can. Create stability and order for yourself and others as much as you can.
3. River: The Blue River represents slow and steady movement towards greater skill and physical means. The change might be imperceptible, but over time you will see how small changes add up to grand differences. Don't get discouraged; growth is an undercurrent and always includes setbacks. Be diligent and keep moving forwards!
4. Waterfall: When misguided, rules, systems, and stability can lock you into paths that damage you and everyone around you. Make sure to question the systems that you're a part of! What are they asking you to do? Where does your loyalty lie, and does whatever you put your faith in merit that faith? Try not to be too rigid in thinking and in your patterns of action: this can also trap you in damaging cycles.
5. Eye: Take stock of all your skills, all your physical resources, of the rules you follow and systems you respect. See how it influences you and the people around you. See the good and the bad in the systems and laws of the world. Inspect the details and examine them as a whole. See how your skills and resources affect how you see yourself and the people around you, and make sure you really *see* the people who have or know less than you.
6. Sea: Be grateful for whatever physical possessions you might have! And if you have enough, be willing to contribute to those around you, both of your skills and of your means. Accept your physical reality and ground yourself in it; understand exactly where your stability lies. Create spaces of safety and invite others into them.
7. Cloud: Everybody has different skills and means, and your working pace might not be the same as anybody else's. This is good! Work at the pace that is best for you and do the best that you can with your own means and skills. There is stability and confidence in knowing your own limits and rules and sticking within them. Also be willing to modify your systems if they aren't serving you! We create rules and systems to protect and guide *us*: we should not be serving our systems.
8. Mountain: You may be feeling unstable, you may not have enough for your needs, or you may be feeling like you lack the skills to gain any sort of success in this life. Keep climbing up the mountain! Let your growth be in the journey, let yourself gain strength and inner stability through the uncertainty. Use this as an opportunity to learn how to be self-sufficient despite your struggles.
9. Dragon: No matter what skills and means you may have, make sure to strive to protect others with whatever you have! Build your life up so that you may give to others and provide a stable foundation so that they may build. Take responsibility for what you *do* have, and try your best to give whatever you feel comfortable giving! Others will benefit greatly from your skills, your means, your protection, your stability.
10. Planet: Stability is strength and strength is an opportunity to protect and offer stability to others. Balance your stability with passion and adaptability, and make sure your skills and physical means are put to good use. Use rules and order in your personal life as tools to enable you to build and give, protect and stabilize. Be dependable in everything you do!

## Green
1. Mote: You have a distinct perspective, one that shapes your intuition into something distinct. Be willing to test out and act on small feelings that may grow into habits, opportunities, or ways that you can serve others. Start small, and be open to new ideas even if they seem illogical at the start.
2. Hands: You have experiences that have shaped you into the person you are today. Let your own intuition guide how you help and lift others around you! Know that your particular viewpoint will make what you create unique, and that your distinctness can move others to act or to change in constructive ways. Be willing to take novel opportunities to serve, even if they come upon you quickly!
3. River: As you change and grow, make sure to stay adaptable! No one can solve all their problems by turning to the same solution. Feel free to try out different approaches if one isn't feeling right; if you trust in your intuition and your latent creativity, growth is sure to come as long as you keep at it. Respect your intuition and your inner compass and make sure that you aren't breaking your own trust for the sake of someone else.
4. Waterfall: Be careful of seeking after too much comfort, or of trusting in only your intuition: both can lead you astray. Make sure that you open your mind to ideas and experiences that might give you new perspective, even if they seem uncomfortable at first. Sensation and pleasure are beautiful but can be a trap if you use them to run away from internal or external problems you may face!
5. Eye: Recognize that your physical desires, your cravings, your need for comfort and sensation can be harnessed for good or for evil! See how others around you have either harnessed these feelings for good or been consumed by them. Witness with frankness the directions your body pushes and pulls you and understand where that could lead you if you give in completely. Recognize the roots of your emotions and inclinations and explore why you react the way you do. What in your past makes you feel this way now?
6. Sea: Accept your past and who you've become because of it. Be grateful for the good and seek to understand and contextualize your hardships. Support others in their struggles and seek to give others comfort in whatever ways you can. The ways that you've changed, whether because of your choices or because of outside circumstances, can be harnessed into vitality, creativity, and experience. Let the lessons you've learned shape you in wholesome ways!
7. Cloud: Understand the uniqueness of your own viewpoint, cherish it and be willing to share it. Don't conform to others' expectations blindly! Cultivate your own style and see the beauty of your own experiences.
8. Mountain: Sometimes your creativity and intuition will lead you down a dead-end path: keep moving! Be willing to adapt and change your approach, but if you feel like the right answer is to keep climbing the mountain, do it! You might feel like you're not going anywhere; just stay nimble and trust in the process.
9. Dragon: Everybody has innate creativity and intuition, and the Green Dragon symbolizes the power that comes from that creativity and intuition. Trust it! Give of your creativity! As you come to take responsibility for your inner voice, you'll learn how to give it to others in ways that will uplift everyone around you.
10. Planet: Look back at the whole of your life and experiences and think about how it's made you who you are. See how you've changed and adapted and ponder whether you're in the right place for you. Respect your past and strive to contextualize and assign meaning to your experiences. Recognize that you must balance your intuition with knowledge and passion! Let yourself reflect your experiences and be true to who you feel you are.

## Gray
1. Mote: Great journeys often start with the tiniest of questions. Identify your own curiosities and delve into them! Plant the seeds of ideas and see which ones grow. Learn how to learn.
2. Hands: You have knowledge to share: share it! Reach out and learn what others know; be willing to teach and listen. Work on better ways to organize and share knowledge and give them to others. Learn about all sides of an issue or topic; understand and consider all types of viewpoints, even the ones that might be misguided, so that you may understand everybody's motivations and needs.
3. River: Understand that knowledge and understanding takes a while to develop and mature, and accept that the path that you are on may be long. Learn and teach as much as you can and your understanding and confidence will grow. Continue to make connections and don't be afraid to ask someone with a more complete understanding than you! Every question asked and answered is a step towards a more whole understanding.
4. Waterfall: Be wary of hubris: the moment you think you know everything is the moment you've lost the plot. Make sure to continue to honestly evaluate your beliefs and the truths you hold infallible. Be careful not to trample others for the sake of your learning, or to jeopardize your safety or the safety of others for innovation and progress.
5. Eye: See both the error and the truth in the world around you! Learn to recognize that lies are often more convenient than truths, and seek to understand how lies propagate and reinforce themselves. Are you lying to yourself? Identify and cleanse yourself of incorrect beliefs and delusion. Witness and embrace the truth even if it's difficult and uncomfortable.
6. Sea: You have knowledge and understanding that not everyone has: be grateful for this! Accept that you understand things in a particular way and seek to understand how your viewpoint differs from that of others. Offer your knowledge and viewpoint to the people around you; help them see what you have seen and experience the things that have helped you in the past. Guide others to the fountains of truth that have sustained you.
7. Cloud: There is beauty and elegance in the truth; sit with this elegance. Be willing to change your mind if you find that the way you understand the world isn't accurate, and see the grace that can come from a change of mind. The way that you see the world is unique, and you have deeply-held truths that others may lack; be willing to share your truths and listen to others' perspectives, even if you think they are wrong.
8. Mountain: Sometimes confusion lasts for long periods of time, even years. Sometimes you will feel like understanding is far, far away, and that you'll never resolve your questions. Take stock of where you are and where your search for knowledge is taking you and change course if necessary. If you feel like you're journey is oriented correctly, keep moving! Sometimes understanding takes a long time to reach. Try to savor the small steps, the little epiphanies that will take you to the top of the mountain.
9. Dragon: Any portion of knowledge grants you power and responsibility; take this responsibility seriously! Give of your knowledge and guide others on their journeys. Share lessons that you've learned and ways that you've innovated freely. Help others make sense of the world around them and you will understand the way things work better and better. Be patient and gentle in your instruction. The Gray Dragon sees, understands, and teaches in equal measures.
10. Planet: Your knowledge is a gift, one that can help guide you personally and in the ways you give to others. Know that your knowledge can only be utilized effectively when you include stability, passion, and intuition into your life as well. Diversify your knowledge and be open to new ideas and questions. You will always be learning! And you might change opinions or learn things that refute facts you thought to be true. Be willing to change your mind!

# God Cards
- Eos, Goddess of Creation: The Goddess of Creation seals wholeness: she sees every part of the creation, every path in the step, and understands it completely. She understands that creation must be in balance in order to achieve true inner peace. Don't let one part of your life overtake any other! Take stock of where you are and how you are feeling and see if anything is out of balance. Passion can overtake you, pleasure can smother you, a desire for stability can chain you down, and a need for understanding can hold you back. Let go of whatever is keeping you from balance!
	- Ethina, Goddess of Order: Study your loyalties, the order of your society and your family, your laws and traditions. Identify the beautiful, constructive order in your own life and around you and build on it! Refine the laws and traditions that you have in order to better serve the people that maintain them and are bound to them.
	- loyalty, order, law, tradition
		- Drahi, God of Gem: Be willing to both share your own skills and accept help from the skilled around you! There is more than enough skill and expertise to go around. Cultivate your own skills and technical knowledge so that you can be a tool both for your own benefit and for the benefit of those around you.
		- Karosh, God of the Earth: Take stock of your life and see the ways in which you are stable or unstable, both physically and emotionally. Seek to leverage your strengths and stabilities into protecting and offering stability to others around you! Think of ways to become more stable and dependable. Protect yourself emotionally and physically so that you can teach and offer that safety to others. Be patient with yourself and others.
		- Valka, Goddess of Metal: Work hard! Work sustainably. Work alone and work together. Productivity can be a beautiful labor of love or can be the simple work of making a living, both are absolutely acceptable. Build just because you want to, build for yourself, build for others, build for your community.
	- Uthala, Goddess of the Living: Take inspiration from the life around you, no matter how lowly or plain. Be adaptable and understand that life is change and change is growth. Be willing to sit in imperfection and uncertainty and still be grateful for who and where you are.
		- Ulagon, God of Plants: Take the small seeds of ideas, of opportunities, of passions and grow them. Nurture the people around you and help them develop into their potential. See the beauty in growth and change and understand that life is meant as a process, not a goal. See the opportunities for growth and cultivation around you, no matter how infertile the ground you've been planted in.
		- Zost, God of Flesh: There is beauty in pleasure and comfort: make sure that you keep some space in your life for relaxation and pleasure. Honor the intuition of your body and emotion and make sure that you aren't shutting your inner voice out for any reason. Make time to sit with yourself quietly, just to be present in your body and your mind. Moderate your consumption and make sure you respect your body and its needs. Your instinct is powerful: listen to it.
		- Yi'yuggatha, Goddess of Strange Creatures: Understand that every creature has their own unique design that helps them thrive in where they've been placed. You have the same uniqueness! Find creative ways to capitalize on the person you are, and lean into your constructive differences: your path is as unique as you are. See the wild variety of plants and animals and realize that inspiration can come in many forms and from many locations!
	- Reina, Goddess of the Stars: Whatever good drive and ambition you have, let it grow! Ambition is only dangerous if directed at the wrong goals or using the wrong means. Examine where you are headed and tweak your goals if necessary. Pick dreams that will fuel you and make your life fill with meaning; they don't have to be large or seem ambitious to anybody but you. Choose dreams that will challenge you, break them down into more achievable steps, and then get started!
		- Iai, Goddess of Light: Let your energy, no matter how bright it may be, shine through everything you do! Let your enthusiasm infuse your actions, your service, your inner world. Be active in finding new ways to learn, new ways to grow, novel paths to take. Share your energy with others! They may need it desperately.
		- Vorash, God of the Flame: Cultivate and give of the warmth of your own soul. Make sure that you're taking time to keep your own flame bright; if you're giving too much you begin to lose your own warmth. Everyone has something to contribute, and when you give to others your own flame of generosity and warmth only grows, as long as you're not burning yourself out. Give of your emotions, your time, and your physical means, if you can.
	- Xoz, God of the Unknown: You cannot know everything! Respect the unknown, especially the unknowns that you're not even aware of. Keep learning, but always keep in mind how much you don't know! This will put your knowledge into perspective and grant humility. Be patient and gentle with yourself as you understand your path.
		- Niorda, Goddess of the Wind: You have curiosities, interests that you've always had but never explored. Identify them! Explore them! Novel and important ideas can come from the most unassuming of places. Don't reject information that comes from outside of your frame of reference; open your mind and see what it has to say! Explore outside of your comfort zone, both intellectually and physically. Let yourself fly.
		- Doliina, Goddess of Water: Give yourself time to introspect: let your mind flow and your thoughts pool, and witness what happens. Journal freely and honestly. Let your well of self-understanding and self-compassion fill and overflow.
		- Qo, Goddess of Entropy: Endings are beginnings, and change is just an opportunity to reevaluate. The universe is constantly tugging us towards entropy and destruction, so it's even more important that we keep putting the work in so that we can be proud of what we've done at the end of it all!

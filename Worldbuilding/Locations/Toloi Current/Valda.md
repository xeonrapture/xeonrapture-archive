# Intro

High above, crowded out by soaring towers of steel and glass, is a sliver of sky, bright blue, mockingly beautiful.

You live in the middle strata of Valda, a messy, sprawling web of streets, farms, shops and clubs, eking out your miserable existence by bounty hunting and taking dirty gang jobs. 

The only things that bring you joy are Nerve, a Lyon drug that makes you feel like you're one with a sunrise you'll never see, soaring through the bright blue sky, free at last; and those moments where you lose yourself in adrenaline: fighting a mob crony to the death, racing through narrow alleyways on a hoverboard, using your xenic powers to pull off a heist.

You save up enough for a cybermod you've been looking forward to, a weapon implant in your arm that could blow a hole through a wall, and smile. Maybe someday you'll make it high up in a powerful gang, or, even, better, find a way to escape this concrete prison, fleeing the gangs and the corpos for good.

But you can't let yourself think those hopeful thoughts. Today you have a job to do, a bounty to catch, a brawl to win.

## Description

Current: Toloi
Danger Level: High 
Native Mortal Species: Humans
Size: equal to Earth
Population: 5 Trillion
Population Density: Insanely Packed
Immigration Rate: High

Valda is a planet completely saturated with skyscrapers and twisty, maze-like buildings of steel and concrete, from the top of the stratosphere to deep, deep into the planet's crust. Ruled by chief executives and policed by gangs and enforcers who all unite to keep the workers, the little people completely under their thumb, working them to the death and using their dreams and needs to fuel their empires.

The High Valdan Council keeps all the corps of the planet bound together with an uneasy peace, only necessary because of strong off-world presences that seek to take advantage of their divisions. The corps prefer to undermine each other subtly, or fight proxy wars through their colonies far away from this planet.

But don't get it twisted, the council is full of figureheads, a farce of true representation. The corps control the planet completely, and only suffer the gangs their power because they keep the people divided. It wouldn't do to have the people below them uniting and seizing control; on a Valdan CEO's path toward supercluster domination, nothing must wake the masses up, nothing make them realize their power.

## Aesthetic and Vibes

The vibe of Valda is very cyberpunk, with soaring, glittering skyscrapers, floating platforms, and space stations in the upper strata, a maze of messy concrete and steel for the middle class in the middle strata, and the barren pits of hell in the lower strata for the dregs of society. See [[#Class and Strata]] below for details on how Valda is structured.

People on Valda have access to both high, chrome, sophisticated tech and "low" tech, all crude and functional, and everything in between. Tech, clothing, and other commodities are made of all sorts of metal, plastic, rubber, and synthetics, often with embedded, glowing elements.

Many people of both higher and lower classes are cybermodded extensively, whether for mostly aesthetic, class-signaling reasons or entirely practical ones. High-up corpos often fuse completely with technology by putting their brain into a robot body or even getting rid of the bio component entirely by transferring their spirit into one. 

See [[Cybermods]] for a list of common mods, or make your own (click [[Xenic Substances#Tech Substances|here]] and [[Xenic Substances#Common|here]] for lists of xenic substances that can be wielded with technology). 

Most everybody you'll find on Valda is individualistic, apathetic, hedonistic, or some mix of all three.

# Worldbuilding and Lore

## Class and Strata

### The Upper Strata

The richest people in Valda live in the upper strata: the skyscrapers themselves, the penthouses at their peaks, in floating platforms and personal space stations far above the land of Valda. They completely control almost all of the farms in the middle strata and the robot and commodity factories of the lower that make everything anybody could ever want, an immense surplus that will never feed those that need it on Valda but instead fund the war machine and colonization efforts across the supercluster. 

Most of the upper strata is penthouses and less-fancy but still-very-fancy apartments, business offices, auditoriums, and entertainment centers for the inheritors of Valdan riches, their underlings, and some lucky social climbers from the middle caste.

Making trouble in the upper strata is a dangerous game: hard to get away with, and if you get caught might just mean being executed or shipped to work a factory in the lower strata.

Transportation here is mostly done in private hovercars.

### The Middle Strata

The middle class (such as it is—the middle class still often needs to scrounge to survive) lives in the middle strata: a complicated structure of interweaving streets, shops, farms, and clubs, with apartments nestled anywhere they'll fit. 

The middle caste performs many important jobs to the economic viability of the Valdan upper class, such as bureaucratic corpo jobs; roles managing the farms of the middle strata and the factories of the lower; entertainment jobs as DJs, dancers, and bouncers; scientific research and technological advancement; and gang hits, drug deals, and weapons shipments. They spend their lives working hard and partying even harder, pursuing vendettas and wasting away on drugs.

One can walk the tangle of boardwalks and bridges, hoverboard or hovercar, or even board an ultrafast train to traverse the middle strata.

### The Lower Strata

The lower class lives in the lower strata: the slums deep in the earth's crust, an inhospitable crevasse of concrete and crude steel made to serve as the foundations of the strata above, to contain the massive factory networks that enrich the corpos, and to house both the technopeasants that work the factories and the enormous prison population. 

The people of the lower caste never see the ground or the sky and eat the trash of the people up above, no advancement or goal to work towards, no purpose for living but to continue existing. They form multiple factions all fighting for territory, over the dumping grounds and the fountains that mean their survival. They create crude gadgets, clothes, and weapons from the refuse of the upper classes, and their cultures are brutal and efficient.

The corpos don't bother with the business of any strata below their own: they know nobody has the power to trifle with them, so they let the common folk have their squabbles and meager treats (as long as these struggles don't affect their bottom line). In fact, they often support gangs and territorial disputes, as these things are much better for the upper strata than the lower classes uniting around their shared oppression.

Paths out of the lower strata are rare and often guarded, and the flying devices that end up there are refuse, unfixable for those who don't have the necessary expertise or parts. Escaping the lower strata isn't unheard of but is definitely not a regular occurrence.

And since Valda was built from the ground up, sometimes adventurers can find fragments of the history of the planet in the lower strata: old libraries, long-forgotten laboratories, buried science, and even occasionally Ancient One ruins.

Most travel is done by foot in the lower strata, walking broken highways and climbing the obtuse walls of the foundations of Valda, though some of the old trains may still be serviceable.

### Other Details

All the architecture on Valda's surface is maintained by a swarm of builder drones, who repair damage and modify the skyscrapers and their sky bridges, maintain and cultivate the buildings of the middle strata, plan and strengthen the complicated foundations of the lower strata, and build new structures as needed. 

Most of the builders in the lower strata are on autopilot, assessing where the foundations might be weak and modifying as needed (which often includes destroying buildings that are being used by the people of the slums in order to protect the skyscrapers above). The builders in the upper strata, however, are managed tightly by the corp in whose territory they reside, and are instructed to build new skyscrapers, skybridges, and other structures as needed. The middle strata is a bit of a mixed bag, not being as explicitly planned as the upper strata but much more protected from wanton destruction and reformation than the lower strata.

Cybermods are extremely common, from the glossy, extremely deadly and effective mods of the corpos above to the brutal, barely-effective mods of the slums below.

Magic and fraternizing with spirits are strictly forbidden and outlawed all across the planet, the punishments for which range from decades of imprisonment and torture to the death penalty. Technotheists say that this is because magic distorts the true, pure nature of the xeon as a resource to increase mortal efficiency; the main reason, however, is that magic gives power back to the people.

## Ecology

Though the planet of Valda has become completely covered in concrete and steel over the ages, that doesn't mean that plants and animals still don't exist. The upper strata are kept clean so all local life ends up in the middle and lower strata.

There's a pretty good amount of moss, vines, and fungus in the middle strata, and it's this plant matter that keeps some species of hardy birds, dogs, and even a couple of types of small, floating, manta-ray-like creatures that have been native to Valda since before the Valdalik Split. 

All of this life only exists in little patches of Valda, isolated from the other sections and slow to spread because of how inhospitable it is. There are still massive swathes of the planet that are entirely barren.

### Flora

There's no soil in the middle strata so no big trees, and the plants that exist on any strata are either xenic species (growing off of metal and stone and absorbing xeon in the air) or tough bio types that seep into cracks in the concrete and get nutrients from water up above or absorb xeon just like the xenic types do.

Deep in the lower strata all the species of moss, vines, and fungus that exist in the middle strata become ever-present in certain areas as long as there's water, covering the concrete completely. There are also tree-like xenic species that grow from the broken stone, their metal tendrils stretching deep to mine for blue xeon and their branches reaching high for any red that may make it down from above.

Plants and other plant-like life exists in the more dry places as well but are much scragglier and even tougher than the other types.

There are no good consistent food sources from these plants, and farming is almost impossible due to the lack of soil.

### Fauna

There are a pretty good amount of species of animals that exist in many (but not all) places of Valda, scrounging up trash or eating the plants that grow on the concrete walls, but really only in the middle and lower strata.

Some more common ones are several species of floating manta-ray-like creatures that eat plant matter, several types of birds that scavenge and eat berries and bugs, some dog- and cat-like creatures that mostly scavenge and have learned how to clamber up and down the strata, some climbing lizards and geckoes that eat mostly bugs, and even some mischievous monkey-like creatures that are really hard to catch.

It's been long enough and the evolutionary selective pressure has been intense enough that there are no living species that closely resemble anything that exists on Ydilik (so nothing should feel too close to Earth animals and plants, which is what much of the life on Ydilik feels like). Everything that exists on Valda can climb very well or fly, as over the years the planet has transformed into an extremely vertical concrete jungle.

In the lower strata the rats have become so big due to all the food waste and their adaptability that over the years they're the size of short humans and now walk on their hind legs and have developed complex language. There are even some reports of them gaining basic xenic powers or creating simple xenic tools. See [[#The Rats]] below.

## Upper Strata

The corps and their minions control the upper strata almost completely, with whole skyscrapers being erected or modified overnight at the whims of the CEOs.

### Corps

Valdan corps wield political power and hold total sovereignty over their territories. Each corp controls large sectors of the upper strata and portions of the middle and lower strata below, where they put their factories and any other less-important buildings.

#### Krangor Tech

Motto is "our victory is your victory".

Krangor Tech is the kingpin of the supercluster, a military tech corp whose mining and factory colonies spread to the far reaches of the universe. Led by Ulysses Krangor, it rules with an iron fist, exercising political and military power over all its subjects with impunity. 

It specializes in heavy, powerful, nigh-indestructible mechs, weapons, and robots; military genius AIs that coordinate war efforts lightyears away from Valda; and teams of agents to take out targets more illicitly.

Its sectors on Valda are some of the most brutalist and worst for the lower classes; Krangor cares about unimpeded imperial expansion across the supercluster and complete military dominance, and all of their aims are bent to that goal.

Their middle and lower strata are more ordered and disciplined than the others, and are patrolled frequently by heavily-armored and -gunned drones that keep everyone in line.

#### Ailith

Ailith is a corp whose chief executive, Uli Ailith, is worshipped as a god by a transmortalist cult and whose specialty is cybermods, robots, and breaking boundaries with AI. Their robots are extremely complicated and advanced, some looking 100% lifelike, with synthetic skin and realistic personalities, and others moving beyond an appearance of biological life into the realms of the perfect robot body.

The Ailith cult (who call themselves The Awakened or the Ailith Transmortalist Collective) and the entire Ailith upper-class are obsessed with becoming one with technology, with each member of the high management of Ailith being as close to pure cyborg—also known as being "purebody"—as they can be. Uli Ailith himself used to be completely human but has since been cybermodded to the point where he doesn't have a human part left—even, if the rumors are to be believed, replacing his brain with a much superior cyberbrain. 

The Ailith upper-class looks down on biological beings as lesser, having left them behind in pursuit of technological immortality. Some worship Uli as their prophet and savior and others are technotheists, believing that technology and technological progress itself should be worshipped. A very small minority worship Dovax, the Goddess of Science, or Exidath, the God of Technology.

Uli Ailith owns a private species of replicants, altered clones of himself who exist just to serve him and the Ailith high class with whatever they may need, be it simple household chores or political assassinations. These replicants are pale and demure, with tightly-controlled lifespans and no sovereignty of their own. A species of cast-off Ailith replicants, completely mindless and wracked with pain, multiply and ravage the castes of the lower strata. There's pretty well-founded rumor that these replicants were created with the support of an ex-E.G. Bio scientist that may or may not be being held captive.

#### E.G. Bio

A smaller, less-powerful corp, Elgin Graham Bio (named after the two founders) is entirely focused on biotech, clones, and biological engineering. Their mech parts and cybermods are entirely biological, with hard carapace or leathery skin for protection and extremely strong melee capabilities.

They make commercially-available replicants for the upper and middle class, servants that will do your bidding and are often specialized for whatever their purpose might be: some are made to pleasure their owners, others are made for household tasks, others are bodyguards, and some are made with the violence and combat ability fit for mortal combat. For the highest fees they'll even make children from your DNA and fit to your specifications (even down to what xenic powers the kids have).

They are known for studying creatures that take their fancy, and will bring novel species back from all around the supercluster, whether they be beast or humanoid. Some of their tortured experiments have escaped and either been killed as they attempt to destroy their makers or disappeared into the lower strata.

#### Estridi

A corp that makes luxury goods for all the richest people around the supercluster; in a twist of irony it's named from the light goddess of beauty, Estrid. The Estridi executives are made up of the leftovers of a royal Valdan family from before the corps took over political power on Valda, whose name has been almost entirely lost to history.

Estridi is obsessed with pure blood: the chief executive's entire bloodline is made up of extremely long-living replicants created from their DNA, and they have bred out any xenic powers as a rich-person flex. 

They also do not cybermod themselves, and do not have any natural-born humans within the family, only replicants created with their own closely-guarded bioengineering technology (obtained by poaching a high-up E.G. Bio scientist). They even keep a stock of their own cloned organs that they use to keep themselves 100% healthy (or at least they try, there are rumors that Estridi executives are frailer than they let on).

They wield a lot of political power and have a extremely subtle and subversive spy and assassin network across all of Valda. Their chief executive structure mirrors royalty structures of old, with obsession over blood and a sophisticated and complicated power succession.

There's a rumor that even Estridi cannot escape the inevitable conclusion of inbreeding, even though Estridi has no natural birth: that several replicant children of high-up Estridi executives didn't turn out as well as they had hoped, and have been banished to the lower strata or worse. 

#### Oigari Corp

Originally based in the Kotharak Field, Oigari Corp is the foremost logistics and transportation company in the supercluster (as long as you leave out the vertically-integrated logistics departments of the larger corps such as Krangor and Ailith). They deal with much of the transportation of goods to and from off-world locations, and ship most of the products from less-powerful companies.

#### Chlorocore 

Is responsible for developing, cultivating, and growing most of the food for Valda's massive population. It controls most of the farms in the middle strata as well as a massive space station the size of a small moon that orbits the planet and whose sole purpose is growing food for the Valdan upper class.

Its more advanced farms grow massive numbers of very large plants and animals by pumping xeon through large emeralds, which in turn emit a powerful green xeon that promotes the life force of these beings. The less-advanced farms that supply the needs of the middle strata mass-produce common, cheap plants and produce them into a large variety of food products that don't necessarily taste good but have enough nutritional value to keep the people working hard.

In recent years some Chlorocore factories have been plagued with mutated plants that have spread and made certain sections of the middle strata uninhabitable. Their unrestrained use of green xeon has even mutated the workers at times, creating unholy, pain-wracked monstrosities that have run amok in the middle and lower strata.

#### Halo Drugs

A pharmaceutical company responsible for many of the performance-enhancing, pain-numbing, and disease-eradicating drugs that keep the high and middle classes healthy. They're always experimenting with new formulations, and even have some extremely expensive drugs that have xenic effects that leave their chemical counterparts behind.

There's a rumor floating around that Halo is just the legal and reputable arm of the Strobe gang.

## Middle Strata

The corps are the ultimate authority on Valda, but gangs and cults run much of the middle strata, dealing drugs, inciting fervor, and distracting the masses.

### Gangs

#### Lyon

Lyon started as a regular gang shuffling around drugs until they found some actual scientists who started helping them make their stuff more and more addictive.

Today they're an interstellar drug-trafficking organization with untold power, only suffered by the corps because they often work together. They have control over swathes of the middle strata and footholds in the vast majority of off-world Valdan colonies, with some of their drugs even making their way to Ydilik under different names.

They're heavily armed and very organized, brutal to their enemies and loyal above all else.

#### Strobe

Strobe is the fun gang, dealing more with psychedelics and other crazy experiences than Lyon does. They run a massive string of rave clubs all throughout the supercluster and often don't beef with other gangs and corps (at least not outwardly). In fact, most people around the universe know them more as an entertainment giant than as a gang.

Likes to have a large amount of blackmail on hand for all the politicians and executives they may want to bribe, and has a large network of [[Webrunning 2.0|webrunners]] going around, stealing important data and sabotaging their enemies.

Huge proponent of [[#Hedonistic Spiritualism|hedonistic spiritualism]].

#### Riot

Riot is an anarchist gang that started as an organization with clear political goals and quickly inspired a massive amount of splinter groups that adopted all the aesthetics and none of the politics of the original.

These splinter groups are anarchists in the colloquial sense of the word: extremely violent, anti-government and chaotic just for the catharsis of it all. Riot is uncontrollable and impossible to completely destroy.

#### The Rai-Kal Syndicate

A rtolka-run syndicate that trades in state secrets and experimental weapons; constantly in territory disputes with other gangs.

#### Gondo Nation

An unruly pbosh gang that has taken the name of a pbosh fascist state. Very violent, uncompromising, and pbosh supremacist (racist against everyone but especially against rtolka).

#### Valdan Resistance Front (VRF)

Group of rebels that started on Valda and has since expanded to resisting Valdan corpo influence around the supercluster. Quite strong but not nearly strong enough to face the corps head-on.

Has safehouses throughout Valda but no dedicated territory because they're literally a revolutionary force that fights against Valdan tyranny everywhere they can.

Is opposed to all the corps and most of the gangs. Designated a terrorist group by the Valdan High Council because they're constantly doing anti-corp terrorism to undermine Valdan power.

### Cults and Religions

#### Ailith Transmortalist Collective

Worships Uli Ailith, CEO of Ailith, as the God of Transcendence and seeks to replicate his transmortalist immortality by replacing as much of their bodies with tech as they can.

Full of mortals in every stage of their transmortalist journey, from a person experimenting with cybermods to brains in a jar to a mortal spirit housed within a fully robot body.

One of their missions is to figure out how to reliably create robots with full spirits like mortals have: they've had some one-off success with this but haven't managed to do it on purpose.

They often trick people into the cult and forcibly modify their bodies, using them as test subjects for their newest and most experimental cybermods.

#### Hedonistic Spiritualism

Takes the drug and party culture that exists all across the planet and turns it up to eleven: you only exist for pleasure, and anything that holds you back from sating your appetites should be shunned or destroyed.

Spread by the drug dealers and the pimps (who, hypocritically but inevitably, don't partake in the self-indulgence nearly as much as their customers do).

More extreme sects of this philosophy take their pleasure in the most depraved carnality, and go around killing, raping, and torturing anyone they can get their hands on, though in the middle strata these people are kept in check by the gangs and in the upper strata they're annihilated.

#### CEO Worship

Wherever there is a powerful CEO there will be a group of people that straight-up worship them as a god, especially when that CEO has become quasi-immortal or augmented their body to the point where they don't even look mortal anymore.

## Lower Strata

### Factions

The factions of the lower strata are loyalty groups that sometimes control territory but more often manifest as disparate but interconnected splinters. The vast majority of these are made up of non-workers, those cast off from the factories or from up above because of bad behavior or simply because they're not useful anymore.

#### Rust

Rust has some of the best mechanics in the lower strata, which means they are actually able to cobble together working vehicles, weapons, and cybermods from the technological detritus that falls from up above. In the end everything they make is very low tech, and the best they can hope for is something that doesn't break down every week.

Mostly comprised of cast-off factory workers, some of which were forced to get crude cybermods specialized to their factory work, such as saw-blade arms or lifting legs.

Pretty good at working together between smaller groups of rust and even other factions in the lower strata but still have disputes or even skirmishes from time to time.

One of the more prominent factions, and an integral part of the lower strata being livable at all.

#### Wanderers

The wanderers are non-violent bands of nomads that move throughout the lower strata, scavenging from the most popular dump sites, solving disputes, and trading their wares. They have the most competent healers in the lower strata, even performing semi-complicated surgery on those that need it (though results can be mixed because of the lack of cleaning and medical supplies).

Not even technically a faction, one band of wanderers doesn't owe any loyalty to any other band of wanderers, it's just a name they take upon themselves because they want to live non-violently and nomadically.

#### The Fist

The Fist is a disparate collection of militant groups run by warlords who impose their will on the lower strata with impunity. They're typically obsessed with obtaining and keeping territory, and will often raid and annex neighboring colonies.

It's truly impossible to live peacefully next to the Fist; one group of the Fist can't even meet another group of the Fist without fighting them.

##### The Bloody Fist

A sub-sect of the Fist is the Bloody Fist: these people are self-destructively warmongery, doing everything they can to massacre, torture, and destroy anything good that can be found in the lower strata. They're identifiable by their self-inflicted scars, blood-painted skin, and yells of barbaric joy as they do their horrible work.

There are rumors that they've been driven mad by the conditions, but they're too organized to be truly mad; another rumor is that they worship Qzoth, Dark God of War, or even G'nolog, the Primordial God of Destruction.

Whatever their motive, when people learn of the Bloody Fist moving into the area they run, or else their sanity and their life is forfeit. The Bloody Fist is known to cannibalize anyone they can get their hands on, often before they're even dead.

#### Ozo

Ozo, or the ozokazi, is a cult that worship the [[xr/Worldbuilding/Dangers/Ozokaz|ozokaz]], a massive, ever-growing AI and robot entity that infects robots and machinery and makes them go violent.

Some of these ozokazi are mentally unwell, many have infected cybermods, and some just see a group of vulnerable and emotionally unstable people that they can manipulate.

Their end goal is to infect all machinery and usher in a new era of ozokaz domination. They are allied with a swath of corrupted robots and even some ozokaz AIs that live exclusively on the web, and will work with them to spread their gospel.

The ozokaz virus spreads unpredictably, though there are devices and software tricks one can use to insulate tech from being infected. In general, the most intelligent robots and AI are better at fighting off the ozokaz virus.

#### Slumlords

Most places in the lower strata that aren't taken up by factories or completely barren are inhabited by groups of castoffs doing the best they can to survive, and every so often one of these colonies gets under the spell of a slumlord, a tyrant who uses the horrible conditions of the lower strata and some scapegoat to gain power over their subjects.

These people can be powerful, ruthless, vindictive, and anything other horrible trait that can be seen in mortals but more so, as the degraded conditions of the lower strata heighten depravity and desperation.

### Threats

#### The Ali-zon

A hoard of mutant Ailith replicants that consume everything organic and kill anyone they can get their hands on. They often vaguely resemble mortal species, as their genetics were initially the same, but have since grown out of control, sprouting arms, eyes, and teeth anywhere their body could put them.

The most dangerous of these are massive fleshy conglomerates, obelisks of flesh and blood, snake- or bear-like behemoths heaving their mangled bodies through the strata.

There's a myth that the ali-zon were mutated by Gorr, the Primordial God of Overgrowth and Cancer, as punishment for making replicants in the first place, but primordial gods don't subscribe to the same ideas of morality that the mortal species do, so that's probably not quite right.

#### Rogue Robots

Among the scrap that rains down from the higher strata are sometimes malfunctioning robots, twitching, trigger-happy, or just plain malicious. These bots wander the lower strata, the more intelligent ones repairing themselves, the less intelligent eventually going dark from lack of repair.

Some of these robots are ozokaz, infected by the ozokaz virus and eyes glowing with the characteristic red, but some aren't, just machinery that is no longer needed.

Very rarely one may stumble upon an Ancient One robot guarding an old stone tomb or ancient ruins, but these are exceedingly rare and only exist deep in the lower strata.

#### Devils

Click [[xr/Worldbuilding/Dangers/Devils|here]] for more info on devils.

Devils are spirits coalesced from strong emotion, xeon formed into a being as a mirror of the feelings of a mortal, and Valda is not a happy place. As such, there are devils all throughout Valda, though their existence is covered up as something magical.

Some of these devils stay in the upper or middle strata, but many of them make their way to (or are created in) the lower strata, where they wander, acting out their emotion until they bond with a human or are exorcised by the rare wandering mystic or cleric.

These devils can be of lust, greed, hate, violence, envy, or even joy or protectiveness (though these are more rare). Click [[XRRPG 1.0 Sorcerer|here]] for more details on what devils are and how to design a devil.

#### The Rats

See [[#Fauna]] above, massive intelligent humanoid rats that live in tribes and take territory. Pretty secretive and can be reasoned with (some groups are more violent than others).

The worst of these worship [[The Motic Pantheon (Dark Gods)|the Dark Gods]] (especially Txazuk), the best work together with people against the other threats in the lower strata.

# Notable Locations

## POIs

Or Places of Interest.

### Goe Portal Hub

On Valda there's a massive portal hub in the middle strata that connects Valda to all its colonies around the supercluster. It's not used for any industrial purposes, only people.

Actively destroying the buildings around it because of how intense the portals are; the amount of purple xeon they use all day every day makes the weather unpredictable and has eroded the concrete around it to the point that they've cleared the buildings around where it was initially placed and constructed massive iron girders underneath it that suspend it far away from the lower strata below.

### Estridi Skyscraper

[[#Estridi]] doesn't control really any territory on Valda except one monument: the Estridi Skyscraper, a building that soars above all the skyscrapers around it and plunges all the way through the middle strata and deep into the lower strata. 

It holds the entire Estridi manufacturing chain (mostly in the lower strata), all the mid-level bureaucrats and their living spaces (in the middle strata), and high-level strategists, officials, and "royal family" (upper strata).

### Valdan Catacombs

Deep beneath the lower strata lays the Valdan Catacombs, a sprawling spiderweb of tunnels carved into the bedrock, some deep underwater, others swarming with unnatural creatures from the distant past. They're extremely difficult to get into and even harder to get out of.

While wandering the catacombs one might run into a hidden chamber full of old treasure or xenic artifacts, a settlement that was used by human political castoffs and runaways, or even an [[Ancient Ones|Ancient One]] shrine, monument, or stronghold.

## Valdan Zones

These areas are big sections of Valda grouped by weather or other distinctive features, and each is split up into sectors based on who controls it (with some sectors straddling the boundaries between zones).

### Clear Zone

A very large zone distinguished by great weather, constructed lakes, parks, and walkable roads in the upper strata, and a middle strata almost completely cut off from the sun.

Comprised of a very large amount of sectors, as each corp wants a slice of this glorious pie.

The most striking part of the Clear Zone is the Heavenly Pulpit, a huge, gleaming space station in low orbit where all the Ailith leadership lives.

### Canopy Zone

Run by Krangor, this zone is characterized by a massive canopy that shadows all the cityscape below.

This canopy covers the entire zone, the space of a small continent, and looks like a big three-sided tarp anchored to the ground by ten-mile-wide metal chains and falling up due to its artificially reversed gravity. On the underside of the canopy is a city, skyscraper fingers reaching down towards the upper strata below it, sparkling multicolored lights twinkling above the Valdan concrete jungle underneath.

The majority of the most powerful Krangor officials live in this canopy, including Ulysses Krangor, who lives at the very top of the canopy in a mostly-glass penthouse that pokes out, surveying all of Valda from above and upside down, the expanse of space stretching below it.

The zone underneath the canopy is depraved, thoroughly run by Lyon, and even the upper strata is utterly destitute and waiting on the Krangor royalty above for approbation or a ticket into the canopy above.

### Cloudy Zone

The Cloudy Zone covers most of Valda, and its name is descriptive: for the vast majority of the year almost all of Valda is covered by a thick layer of clouds that makes the richest and most powerful flee to live anywhere with better weather.

Rain in the upper strata turns into mist and mini-waterfalls in the middle strata, and all that turns into flash-flooding, huge waterfalls, and strange weather patterns in the lower strata.

#### Aiurr Sub-Zone

This sub-zone is named for the regional chemical processor company that owns most of it and is plagued with horrible acid rain for most of the year. People have had to adapt, relying on rubber and other acid-resistant materials to keep their homes and places of business safe.

In the lower and bits of the middle strata the acid has caused horrible diseases and mutations among the bio-life.

#### Flooded Sub-Zone

A small-nation-sized part of the Cloudy Zone hasn't been constructed correctly and so retains all the water coming from the heavens, making a huge, deep lake that goes from halfway up the middle strata all the way down to Valda's bedrock, miles and miles below. 

Citizens use submarines or rely on watertight buildings to live beneath the water level, and an untold amount of secrets are concealed in the depths (seeing as deep water is a much better protector of secrets than space or even the more obscure corners of the [[xr/Worldbuilding/Lore/The Gray Web|gray web]]).

### Nuii Zone

This zone is named for the meteor that destroyed the upper strata and much of the middle strata in this zone. Since then the gangs have moved in and now it's absolute anarchy.

A large part of what was left in the middle strata was a web of highways that are now exposed, they've been built out and are now a huge, messy settlement built on top of and from the bottoms of the highway skeletons.

The culture of this zone is intertwined with the gangs that rule it (a bit of every gang plus a bunch of local ones fighting for territory) and a fast-spreading brand of [[#Hedonistic Spiritualism|hedonistic spiritualism]] that worships [[The Motic Pantheon (Dark Gods)#Uulith-btha|Uulith-btha]].

The zone is rampant with pests and diseases all throughout but especially in the lower strata: in some places it's so bad that the reason people are hedonistic is because they're terminally ill.

### Broken Zone

The Broken Zone is dominated by the source of its brokenness: the rich and powerful in this area got sick of all the clouds, so they made a massive tower that stretches into the heavens and outfitted it with experimental technology to try to manipulate the weather.

Their efforts were disastrous. The upper strata have been completely destroyed by hurricanes, tornadoes, and extra-destructive xenic lightning. The rain never runs out, the wind never stops, and powering anything in this zone is a losing battle due to how much the tower interferes with any technological xenic effect.

The middle strata are unlivable but areas of the lower strata are just barely hospitable: you still may be swept away by winds or floods, a tornado might destroy your home, or you may be burned to death by a web of lightning.

### Quarantine Zone

This zone has been completely walled off; the diseases that infest much of the middle and lower strata of Valda have run totally rampant in the Quarantine Zone, to the point where the pathogens here are completely resistant to medicine and xenic healing and are able to ravage any biological body (and even some cybermods) without killing the host.

All the people should have died off here decades ago if it wasn't for palapin (also known as The Preserver), a xenic prion that keeps your body alive even as you beg for death: it's even able to heal critical injuries if it means you continue to house it in your body.

## In Orbit

### Valda Trash Moon

Much of the refuse of Valda drops down into the lower strata, but for industrial waste that's not feasible as it would end up eroding the foundations of the planet, so instead they send it to the Trash Moon.

It's a faraway moon, orbiting on the opposite side of Valda from Ydilik and leagues away. The corps portal all of their worst trash right onto the moon face which has rendered it toxic and almost uninhabitable.

The only people living on the Trash Moon are the worst Valdan prisoners (or the ones that pose the most threat to the corps themselves). Since there is no atmosphere they have to figure out how to generate it themselves, and pieces of food are extremely few and far between.

There are little colonies on the Trash Moon where groups of castoffs do their best to survive in an inhospitable place, relying on their own powers and their ingenuity and trying to work together.

### Oo Spaceport

The big Valdan spaceport that takes in most of the incoming industrial shipments is called Oo, and it's where all the heavy-duty portals are managed and maintained. It receives a huge amount of warpran goods so it uses an ingenious technological system that utilizes xenic fields to slow down the big rigs and catch them right out of their warprun.

This spaceport doesn't handle the more specialty shipments that corps typically run through their own spaceports, and it doesn't handle any passenger transportation (see [[#Goe Portal Hub]] above).

### E.G. Spaceship

The house of [[#E.G. Bio]], a smaller but still influential corp involved in biological engineering. The experiments that take place on this ship are kept secret, but the results can't be argued with.

# Gods on Valda

Not many light gods are active on Valda because the mortals there don't listen to them (or even believe in them). The majority of Valdan mortals want unrestricted debauchery and greed and don't want the light gods messing up their bag.

That said, [[xr/Worldbuilding/Powerful Beings/Archons/Exon|Exon]] does work with some of the more level-headed scientists to invent and make scientific discoveries, but he has pretty limited influence overall.

Valda is where Exon and a small group of scientists created the mortal species called the dovaxi.

All of the [[The Motic Pantheon (Dark Gods)|Motic Gods]] are active on Valda to a certain extent, but they tend to be subtle because Valdans don't really want to believe that they're in service of a god.

# A Very Brief History of Valda

Valda is one of the original two human homeworlds broken off from Valdalik in the [[Basic XRU History#The First Age (40,102-28,989 PAE)|Valdalik Split]], and they worked with Ydilik to create the first amethyst portal.

Valda starts to become a technological powerhouse around 170 AE and the corporations buy out the nations and city-states of Valda because of their debt to the corpos in 1,375 AE.

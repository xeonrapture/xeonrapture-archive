# Main Coordinate Systems

Many currents, zones, superstructures etc. have different ways of measuring location within space, but there are two agreed-upon ways to measure where one is.

One is current specific and is called Current Coordinate (CC). The other is current-agnostic and is called Isveh Supercluster Coordinate (ISC). Each current has their own code to convey which current someone is talking about when using the CC system.

## Isveh Supercluster Coordinate System (ISC)

![[Isveh Supercluster Coords.png]]

Also known as KTD after the names of the coordinates themselves.

The Isveh Supercluster Coordinate system has three different reference points: the Toloi Current, the Kyypreskar Current, and the Center of the Isveh Supercluster. Each of these have a more specific key, and out of these three keys are derived what is basically the Latitude (NS angle from Center where 0° pointing at the Kyypreskar Current and 180° pointing directly away from it) and Longitude (angle from the Center where 0° points straight at the Varda Star Cluster, and goes up to 180° and down to -180°).

Or, to put it a different way, think of the space that the Isveh Supercluster takes up as a massive sphere: the vertical axis is delineated by the Kyypreskar Current, the horizontal axis is the Toloi Current, and the center of the sphere is the Center, where all Currents flow.

The last key is D, or Depth from the Center.

In general these Keys were chosen because they're easy to read from far away, whether because they have a unique xenic or electromagnetic reading. The UVM-177 Megastar has a very unique and strong xenic and electromagnetic reading, and the Marda Star Cluster (where Valda and Ydilik are located) is constantly emitting strong signal. The Center of the Supercluster is the strongest emitter of red xeon and so is extremely easy to index off of and decipher distance from.

| Key | (Key)  | Range               | Key Current | Specific Key                       |
| --- | ------ | ------------------- | ----------- | ---------------------------------- |
| K   | Y axis | 0° to 180°          | Kyypreskar  | UVM-177 Megastar                   |
| T   | X axis | 180° to -180°       | Toloi       | Varda Star Cluster                 |
| D   | depth  | 0 to 5.6 million AU | ---         | D is distance away from the Center |

K and T are measured in degrees (often with a decimal afterwards for precision) and D is measured in AU, or Amethyst Units, which is how much distance one can travel with one AE. Each AU is 1,000 Light Years.

Each current has a K/T pair that tells you where they are in relation to everything else. 

> [!NOTE] Example ISC Coordinates
> 70° K, -114.5° T, 4.76M AU D (or simply 70°,-114.5°,4.76M)

## Current Coordinate System

![[Current Coords.png]]

Each Current has their own local coordinate system that tells you where you are in relation to the current itself.

| Key | Range              | Meaning                                                               |
| --- | ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| A   | 180° to -180°      | Angle around the current, 0 always points at the UVM-177 Megastar     |
| O   | Max 200K AU        | Distance out from the core of the current, the center red xeon stream |
| Dc  | Max 5.6 million AU | Distance from the Center. Range depends on the Current                |

If we want to be *really* precise, Dc is actually the distance of the nearest point on the center red xeon stream from the Center, otherwise you gain more and more distance from the Center the farther from the core of the current you get. That's why it's notated with Dc and not D, it's a modified "depth from Center" coordinate and not a strict Depth like is used in the ISC system.

The Depth is often switched out for distance upstream and downstream (e.g. "it's 2.3 million AU upstream from our point").

The center stream of a Current changes course very slowly, over millennia. Occasionally the governing bodies will issue minor corrections to everyone's star charts, but mostly what they change is the distances of certain stars as the slowly drift downstream.

> [!NOTE] Example Current Coordinates
> -67.4° A, 12.5K AU O, 3.4M AU Dc (or simply -67.4°, 12.5K, 3.4M)


# Kotharak Field Coordinate System

Here is the coordinate system for the Kotharak Field!

| Key    | Range         | Meaning                                                                       |
| ------ | ------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| A(kf)  | 180° to -180° | Angle around the Kotharak Field, 0 deg. always points at the UVM-177 Megastar |
| O(kf)  | Max 50K AU    | Distance out from the core of the Kotharak Field                              |
| Dc(kf) | 0 to 2.2M AU  | Distance from the start of the Kotharak Field                                 |

> [!NOTE] Example Kotharak Field Coordinates
> 100° A(kf), 12.5K AU O(kf), 3.4M AU Dc(kf) (or simply 100°, 12.5K, 3.4M)

AU are Amethyst Units: one AU is how far you can go with one Amethyst Energy (1K Light Years).

## Notes

- O(kf) is measured from a line that's between 50K and 100K AU away from the core of the Toloi Current at any given time (because the Kotharak field sits at about 24.6° A and between 50K and 100K AU O).
- Dc(kf) is actually picks up from the most up-stream tip of the Kotharak Field, and takes the distance of that tip from the Center and measures down the length of the Kotharak Field from there. This effectively gives the Kotharak Field its own center line, making it easier to navigate (because you won't have to be measuring from the center of the Toloi Current constantly).

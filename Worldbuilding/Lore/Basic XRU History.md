# Intro

It might have been up to several hundred billion years ago when the universe was formed.

Was it created by a god? Was there some sort of big bang, and matter and xeon existed all at once? Or did it just blink into existence?

We don't know. Nobody remembers back that far, not even the gods, and we haven't found any evidence that points any which way.

What we do know is that _our_ history starts around 71,000 years ago, the beginning of the Age of Creation, when the Archons, _our_ gods, began to create the archonic intelligent mortal species.

# Terms

##### Year Designations

**PAE** = Pre-Amethyst Age
**AE** = Amethyst Age

##### God Terms

**Archons** = the nine middle gods ruling over the Archonic species (from eldest to youngest: Ai, Goddex of Wisdom; Toko, God of the Law; Torrena, Goddess of Survival; Vaiana, Goddess of Change; Exon, God of Progress; Pbosh, God of Passion; Rtolka, God of Knowledge; Gatrathki, God of the One; and Osadona, Goddess of the Many)
**Motic Gods** = the seven dark gods led by Mot, the Corrupt One (Ngyer-ein, the Right Hand of Mot; Sveh, Mot's Eyes; Qzoth, God of War; Txazuk, God of Tricks; Uulith-btha, Goddess of Greed; Xotl, God of Madness; and Zvithwa, Goddess of Chaos)

##### People Terms

**Mortal species/intelligent mortal species/mortals** = all mortal people intelligent enough to understand and use complex language; usually humanoid, standing up on feet and using tools with hands, but not always
**Archonic species** = all mortal species created by the Archons
**Specism** = racism but against a different mortal species

# The Age of Creation (70,000-40,102 PAE)

According to legend the aith were first, tall pale white and blue antlered beings, glowing softly, supremely intelligent and restrained; fitting characteristics for a people created by Ai, the First Archon, the Goddex of Wisdom.

Not much is known about the actual process of creating a mortal species[^1], but we do know that the gods approach it carefully, spending hundreds or even thousands of years planning, preparing, learning exactly how a mortal body functions and discerning the deepest intricacies of xenic mastery. Sadly the gods rarely even mention the creation process, and those involved in the creation of the dovaxi have been sworn to secrecy.

After creation the gods often set their mortal species up with a language and some basic technology, teaching them how to manipulate the xeon with powers or even a magical language.

The aith were the first of the archonic species, but they weren't the last: after the aith came the rest of the mortal species we know, including the exoziniks (54,506 PAE), the humans (51,535 PAE), and pbosh (49,665 PAE), all created by their respective Archons and set up to some extend by their gods.

From there these species weren't necessarily on their own but most of their gods opted to take a step back and let these newly-made mortal societies figure things out for themselves. Some Archons lived with the species they made and remained accessible to a certain degree, and others decided to live away, such as in a moon in sight of their species (Exon living on Vilidan Prime), or in The Center, a dense cluster of stars at the center of the supercluster.

Most mortal species were created able to [[XRST Adepts|channel the xeon through their bodies]]; because of this and the proclivity of mortals to kill one another the Age of Creation was extremely bloody. At times this violence even reduced their populations down enough to concern their Archons, who had to step in several times to make sure they didn't war themselves into extinction. In general, though, the mortals were saved from themselves by a lack of resources, their own ineptitude, or some level of restraint.

# The First Age (40,102-28,989 PAE)

This age begins when humans figure out how to use xenic substances to create magical items and artifacts (though by this logic a better starting point would be the kaldi creating their bard magic in 41,877 or even the exoziniks creating their technology in 41,605—you'll soon see that victory is written by the powerful).

In this age the rtolka discover magic (36,778), the pbosh create their own ferrotech (32,933), and exoziniks create spacecraft (35,460); in general this age is defined by baby steps in technological experimentation, a bit of progress in learning the many ways to manipulate the xeon, and even a bit of space travel.

Another important event was the Valdalik War (32416-31534) which ended in the Valdalik Split, where Vaiana saw that two nations of humans were on the cusp of eradicating one another and decided that ripping their planet in half and forming two new planets (Valda and Ydilik) for each of the warring nations was preferrable to humans ceasing to exist.

There aren't too many records of this time, so what is known is based on the memories of immortals and the gods (who tend to have much better memories than mortals, even if the target of their recall was millennia ago).

# The Second Age (28,989 PAE-894 AE)

The Second Age begins as humans discover silver and gold powers which enable them to wage war and consolidate power like they never had been able to before.

This age is defined by rapidly accelerating movement forward in tech and magic, each mortal species focusing on different fields depending on what they care about, their material and environmental conditions, and how violent and power-hungry their species turned out and how successful they were in realizing their violent ambitions and their power hunger.

For example, the kaldi have never been too violent: their bodies aren't as strong as human bodies and not _nearly_ as strong as pbosh bodies. They don't tend to war, instead opting for more subtle violence such as reputation destruction, emotional torment, and assassination.

For another case study, the pbosh were born on a brutal ice planet pockmarked by volcanoes, so their technology is sturdy and environmentally-resistant, enabling them to do very well in their harsh environment.

## The First Pbosh-Rtolka War (27,232-22,305 PAE)

The first inter-species war was the First Pbosh-Rtolka War, a massive, brutal war spanning multiple planets in the solar system that houses both pbosh and rtolka homeworlds.

This was was actually begun by the Archons Pbosh and Rtolka: their own personal grudge led to a series of misunderstandings and retaliations that led to them leading their eponymous species to war.

There are several [[Supercluster Portal Network#Interstellar Lay Line Portals|natural lay line portals]] that connect the planets Pbosh Oo'lo and Rtolka-Dala to one another, a handful of which open regularly enough to accommodate a large-scale war.

About four hundred years into the war the rtolka create retrotech and then figure out space travel which enables them to decide the time and place of their attack on the pbosh planet, greatly accelerating the pace of the conflict (though it wouldn't be over for several more millennia). The pbosh figure out their own version of space travel shortly after.

The gods Pbosh and Rtolka have since buried their own hatchet, but the inter-species grudge is not so easily buried: many or even most pbosh and rtolka across the Isveh Supercluster live peacefully with one another but still harbor some learned, unconscious bias, but many others embrace their generational specism and use it to fuel ongoing scapegoating and violence.

## Technological Advancement (Early Second Age)

In 16,877 the exoziniks discover warprunning and begin mapping and exploring their current (Toloi) and the Kotharak Field.

In 11,539 the humans discover the xenic effects of diamonds, enabling them to supercharge all of their preexisting xenic effects (a discovery that, at the time, made humans genuinely ahead of the curve for once).

The rtolka and the pbosh both discover warprunning and start colonizing their current (Ghashnalah) within almost twenty years of one another (8,433 and 8,415 respectively), leading them to discover Brogollok, a jungle nebula rich in resources, and Wola-bodoma, the walua homeworld. Fairly quickly after this they also discover the kaldi homeworld (named Kaldi).

In 6,997 the humans discover scrying and start sending messages to Valda from Ydilik and vice versa.

## The First Motic War (4,388-4,084 PAE)

The first war against the dark gods started almost as an accident: Qzoth the God of War had been nursing some deep-seated enmity against Vaiana and started taking it out on the humans and the kaldi: finding some more charismatic and war-hungry individuals, indoctrinating them and filling their heads with visions of grandeur and battle-glory, and inciting them to lead their followers to commit bloody massacre on their own people.

This was unacceptable to Vaiana, usually one of the more tender-hearted and kind Archons, who then led most of her fellow Archons to wage all-out war on the Motic Gods, a suite of evil beings hell-bent on killing the Archons and their mortal species.

Both pantheons of gods led their sides, comprised of groups of archonic mortals that had found themselves on either side of the conflict for whatever reason, into massive, multi-pronged conflicts all across most of the populated planets, desperately trying to cripple the other side into submission and only occasionally fighting with their own godly power.

This war was mostly waged through god- and immortal-created portals: either side would make a big portal through which to lead an army, a strike team, a fleet of spaceships, etc. which would then assault whatever stronghold or target they needed to take out. These targets were shielded from portal-creation so that one couldn't drop a team right in the middle of a castle or city, and most existing defensive technology was pioneered during this war, whether it was city-scale shielding or portal-jamming devices.

Watching these powerful beings creating portals almost on a whim inspired certain human scientists and magicians particularly, who created the field that would culminate four thousand years later in the creation and stabilization of portals themselves.

The death toll of this war was staggering, far dwarfing that of even the Pbosh-Rtolka War tens of thousands of years before. Historical casualty and death estimates vary wildly, with some putting the death toll at hundreds of millions and others saying it was as many as several billion. We'll never really know, unless one of the gods counted and decides to tell us.

The war ends with a whimper, a final defeat of the dark gods forcing them to go back into the shadows, but with both sides being so depleted that there wasn't enough muscle to keep a lid on the societal unrest that started as soon as the war ended.

This conflict fundamentally changed how mortals saw the gods: some of the Archons and their lesser gods became famous and racked up selfless deed after selfless deed, truly deifying themselves in the eyes of the mortals; others stayed back and helped with the development of useful technologies or in the psychological battle to win hearts and minds to the cause; and some even fled and weren't heard of until the war ended or beyond.

The Motic Gods, while known of before this time, became the horror story that every parent told their children to get them to stop misbehaving. While they were real and would interfere in the business of mortals before, now they and their deep hatred of all things good and beautiful were an existential threat.

## Between Wars

The time after the First Motic War was not necessarily a peaceful one: the dark gods had been beaten back, but both sides had been so wounded and the art of war had advanced so much that the violence was only reduced to a smolder instead of being put out entirely.

Every planet that had been involved in the war attempted to rebuild and reconstitute their populations, housing, and infrastructure, but too many governments had been dismantled, leaders been assassinated, and economic hubs been crippled for the reconstruction to be anything other than a gargantuan feat under the best of circumstances.

## The Second Motic War (2,671-1,823 PAE)

The Second Motic War would start with the killing of Ophai, the Lesser Goddess of the Harvest, a feat that had never been done. This war was similar to the First but much, _much_ more brutal: the dragons got involved and did a massive amount of damage, as their power matches that of a lesser god; the dark gods invoked several elder gods who participated in the annihilation of whole nations; and some secondary-world colonies are destroyed entirely.

This war ends just like the first: the side of the light gods won but the violence never quite died out, the reconstruction started very slow, and the dark gods kept stirring up conflict.

## Post-Second Motic War

During this time several of the elder gods turn their attention to the Isveh Supercluster which causes both weal and woe, and mortal resistance groups are formed in order to keep the forces of evil never again able to take control of mortal hearts like they did during the two Motic Wars.

The Battle of Gjgrathka took place on Ydilik in the year 363 PAE, and it's here that both Valdan and Ydilikian humans teamed up to turn against the servants of the dark gods that had been trying to bait them into another war against one each other. They won the battle, killed the dark servants, and vowed to sever themselves from the dark gods entirely.

All archonic mortals took heart at this, so the next long stretch of time is marked by relative peace. The Great (Human) Peace Treaty was signed in 42 PAE between all Valdan and Ydilikian governments as an attempt to rely on their own strength more than that of the light gods and to refocus on their own technological and societal progress.

## The First Amethyst Portal (1 AE)

After a massive amount of work and endless tiny technological and magical advancements, a portal was created between Valda and Ydilik, reuniting humans in a way that hadn't been seen since the split of Valdalik.

Before portals the only ways to travel faster than light had been warprunning, which combines red and blue xeon into purple and uses it to partially enter the xenic plane, condensing space greatly; and entering and traversing the xenic plane.

Warprunning—an extremely important technology that's still used today for transporting volatile materials and exploring—is too slow and too resource-intensive to allow for travel between currents, and traversing the xenic plane is much too unreliable and inconsistent a method to allow for day-to-day transport.

Portals unlocked instantaneous travel across massive distances, with the only limiting factors being the (often very large) amounts of red and blue xeon needed to feed the portal and the technologies needed to stabilize it.

After this it only took twenty five years to establish the Interstellar Coalition between Valda and Ydilik, a uniting organization meant to keep all mortal species collaborative and secure.

## Late Second Age (Post First Portal)

During this time there are a massive amount of huge technological advancements, as well as quite a bit of exploration:

- Exoziniks discover zirconium tech, enabling them to send messages with tech across space instantaneously (49)
	- They use this a ton to facilitate exploration of their own current, and it becomes a very important piece of tech as soon as they connect with the humans
- Valda leans more and more into technology (172ish)
	- Not nearly as corrupt as they are in modern times
- Ebalin (a gas giant) and Voroki are discovered by the Interstellar Coalition humans and they begin to colonize them (356)
- Exoziniks start using silicon in computer chips, enabling them to handle logic and store information (721)
- Another Pbosh-Rtolka War (795-800)
	- Much smaller in time period than the first but just as bloody due to massive population growth and technological advancement

# The Third Age (894 AE-Today)

The Third Age begins with the first intergalactic portal, between Valda and Exozina (the exozinik homeworld) which truly heralds a new age for the archonic species. The humans and exoziniks had already been communicating for a while before the portal with zirconium tech, so they even understood one another's languages to a certain extent.

This was the first time humans and exoziniks had seen one another since the Motic Wars (which had been a shock given that most archonic species only knew about other mortal species through the word of the gods and immortals). The Interstellar Coalition expands to include the exoziniks not long after.

In 1,300 the first inter-current portal was created, connecting the pbosh and the rtolka (and by extension the kaldi and the walua) to the rest of the quickly-growing portal network: a massive feat given that the only way to find another mortal species from that far away is to search for strong, artificial xenic signatures from across the supercluster, reach out to them with zirconium tech (and hope they have the gear to accept your message and the knowhow to begin to translate your language), and then figure out the best place to put the portal.

As each of these mortal species are discovered by the Interstellar Coalition and networked in they add a new element to society as they start immigrating and their cultures start mixing together, with all the blessings and problems that come from that (especially given the fact that intra-species multiculturalism is _already_ sometimes hard to deal with and racism exists in as many or even more places than specism does).

## Spirit Anchors Invented (1,325 AE)

Another seismic shift: [[Spirit Anchors|spirit or xenic anchors]] are invented, enabling slightly increased lifespans and the ability to regenerate your body if you get killed.

This leads to a bunch of societal changes, including adventurers (and everyone, really) getting much harder to kill, many people getting much more reckless, and those with lots of resources becoming absolutely obsessed with achieving immortality.

Spirit anchors start extremely expensive and are not cheap even now, so they're favored mostly by adventurers, those that deal in violence, and the very rich and powerful.

## Corruption Grows (around 1,375 AE)

At this point the corporations and governments of Valda and Ydilik were starting to corrupt in every way, in part driven by the realization that with a combination of spirit anchors and other experimental and dangerous solutions their leaders may achieve some form of immortality.

In fact the more power the Interstellar Coalition gets and the more it colonizes around the Isveh Supercluster the more horrible it becomes: the rtolkan high council becomes obsessed with hoarding and gatekeeping knowledge, the fascist state Gondo Nation gains power on Pbosh Oo'lo, and the exoziniks turn their eye more and more to weapons of war.

## The Expansion and Colonization

From here on out the Interstellar Coalition expands at an accelerating pace: the osadona are discovered and folded in around 1433 and the dovaxi (a mortal species made entirely of xenic technology) is created by a group of scientists working with Exon in 1522.

Conflicts with the [[xr/Worldbuilding/Dangers/Vodarak|vodarak]], a quick-reproducing species of bug-people, start around 1572 all around the supercluster due to some IC colonies butting up against their territory, and Jiyyagu, a jungle megastructure several light-years wide, is discovered in 1622.

# Today (1,666 AE)

[The year is 1,666](https://app.xeonrapture.com/xr-tools/xr-clock.html), and things are as bad as they've ever been. Corruption abounds among the powerful, and the wealth gap is cavernous. The light gods are quiet, and the dark gods are ascendant, quietly building their empires, infiltrating the halls of the rich, influencing their pawns towards chaos and violence.

Ozokaz, a massive nation of rogue robots all led by a power-hungry AI, nips at the boundaries of civilized space, infecting our technology, turning our tools against ups. Unrest is universal, and destructive and violent devils pop up everywhere in response.

People turn to drugs, to the gangs, to cults, to piracy, anything to inspire them and make them forget their horrible lot in life.

Where will us mortals end up? Will we destroy ourselves? Or can we rise up, cast off the leeches at our side, and unite against those who would annihilate us? All this remains to be seen.

# Some Additional Notes

So how do we know all this history?

Some of it was written down: each species discovered written language on their own, and over time their recording methods became more and more permanent.

But the oldest information we get is from immortals and even gods, those who have memories of those times and whose memories are sharper than those of mortals.

[^1]: One of the leading theories is that the creation of a mortal species includes accelerating evolution, an existing mechanism that we know exists and has been utilized artificially to mold less intelligent species to our ends. If we can do it over several millennia then the gods can surely do it in less time.

# Intro

Also called the spirit world, the spirit plane or realm, or the astral world or plane.

The xenic plane is a plane of existence that exists parallel to the physical world and is a sort of spiritual realm, inhabited by elemental spirits, the souls of departed mortals (called shades), the spiritual bodies of devils and demons, and a few very lucky (or unlucky) mortals who have lost their way.

It is the realm that the gods and spirits have the most power in--though the gods have no shortage of power in the physical plane as well--and is shaped and molded by the minds of mortals and all intelligent beings.

The xenic plane exists partially independent from physical space; xeon is infinitely compressible, so place is relative there and stretches and folds in surprising ways. It is through harnessing this realm's peculiar properties that people are able to zip through the Isveh Supercluster by warprunning and creating portals.

# What is the Xenic Plane?

The xenic plane is like a great ocean: by travelling on the surface one can reach great speeds, but by delving deeper and deeper one can discover the greatest secrets of the universe, commune with gods and spirits, and witness its wonders and its strangenesses.

The deeper one goes the more abstract and dreamlike things get, and the more tied to the psyches of those traveling through it; though be wary, as the denizens of the spirit world have made their own rules, and breaking them may seal your doom.

## The Depths of the Xenic Plane

The deeper one goes the easier it is to get lost, and there are whole colonies of mortals that've been whisked away into its depths, never to be seen in the physical realm again. Natural portals to the spirit plane exist rarely but can pop up anywhere, and are especially prevalent in places where xenic lay lines cross (such as a powerful stream of red and blue) and rip at the fabric of the physical world.

As the gray web technically exists within the spirit realm, small portals from one to the other have been known to show up in the gray web (though the web was created with a different order in mind and so goes by rules much different from those of the spirit world).

Enchanters and other more mystical casters are the only ones that truly excel at creating portals from the physical world to the xenic one, as physics, scientists, and others with more evidence-seeking minds have a hard time touching the spiritual.

## Entering and Traversing

Getting into the xenic plane can be as simple as stumbling upon a naturally-occurring portal or as complicated as doing a ritual to create a door into it from scratch.

In some places the spirit world and the physical one are close, making traversing the boundary much easier. This can happen because of a strong presence in the xenic realm, but it can also happen because the people believe in and are concerned for the spiritual world or because they have a holy site nearby that they've cared for over the centuries.

Xenic lay lines cross the entire supercluster, sometimes visible, most often not. They are, however, trackable by seers and certain magical and technological xenic instruments, and when they cross they can create natural doors into the xenic plane (this is especially common with red and blue lay lines).

Some of these doors open randomly, others at a consistent time every year or month, and very rarely are open all the time. Occasionally these natural portals open directly to another physical location or even allow dependable travel between two planets through the xenic plane, so before artificial portals and spacecraft were created these portals were used to immigrate to and establish settlements in unexplored areas of the supercluster.

Creating and keeping a portal into the xenic plane open takes _less energy_ overall than an amethyst portal that just _uses_ the xenic plane to get from one physical location to another. [[Enchanter|Enchanters]] of a certain level can make them, and if anyone learns the ritual and gathers the correct components then they can as well.

As far as travelling within the xenic plane, however, it's often just a matter of finding the spirit that knows the way to the right door, having someone in the party that can read the xeon around them so they can point you in the right direction, or locating an object that can guide you through before entering the spirit world at all.

Getting from one place to another is never straightforward, so think on your feet and don't be afraid to leap into the dark! Often the xeon itself will prepare a path for those who are willing to be swept away by it.

# Why Enter the Xenic Plane?

So why roam the xenic plane when you might get lost, bound, or even have your spirit severed from your body?

## Escape

Though the risk of death does exist in the xenic plane it is significantly safer for your physical body, so escaping into the spirit world can be a way to protect yourself from enemies or natural disaster.

When in the xenic plane your aging slows down as well, so people can live out eons tucked away in some pocket plane, away from prying eyes but unable to interact fully with the physical realm.

## Travel

Warprunning and portalling are the most popular ways to travel long distances in the physical realm (both of which utilize the xenic plane to warp space), but if one doesn't have access to a spaceship or the money to use a portal they can use the spirit plane to get from one place to another.

Travelling through the xenic realm can be perilous, and it's very easy to get lost without a guide, but this mode of travel can be the right choice for when one wants to go long distances without having their movement tracked.

## Dealing with Spirits

Sometimes the actions of spirits in the spirit world will affect the physical one or they'll enter and cause havoc in the physical realm directly. In cases like these you might need to enter the xenic realm to go after them and bind them, appease them, or weaken them deeply.

Other times you'll be facing great trials in the physical world and need to ask a powerful spirit or a god for help! Some good spirits were bound ages ago and can be unchained to help deal with some great evil.

To bind or harm a great spirit it can be useful to find their True Name, which may be discoverable only by going to their place of origin, finding an object they imbued their name with, or asking another spirit that they used to trust.

Rarely you'll be tasked with destroying a spirit entirely! This might take long days of preparing the correct components, marking the ground and landscape with ritual glyphs and lines, and luring the spirit to the right place.

See [[Rituals]] for powerful spiritual effects that can affect even the strongest spirits.

## Finding Important Items

There are a multitude of powerful magical items hidden or lost in the xenic plane, many of which can assist you in your journeys in the xenic and physical realms.

You might need to track down a being that can help you create the item you need or strengthen an existing one to the requisite level.

Some items might exist only in memory, and you need to perform the difficult **Dive** [[Rituals#Dive|ritual]] to enter their past to retrieve them.

## Information

People, spirits, and gods have been secreting away information in the xenic plane for millennia, so who knows what you might stumble upon? You can also spy into the physical realm from the xenic plane, or attempt to remember something you were cursed to forget.

## Fix Physical Problems

Sometimes spiritual issues will leak into the physical realm (such as a spirit war or the influence of a dark god). You may need to enter the spirit realm to destroy or bind a great evil, appease elementals so they stop disturbing the elements and creating natural disasters, or shape the xenic plane in order to shape the physical one.

## Fix Spiritual Problems

Higher levels of spirit decay cannot be cured by anything less than a ritual or a god, and in these cases you may need to venture into the spirit realm to find a cleanser.

Curses can affect you both physically and spiritually and can also be cleansed by all sorts of holy sites, spirits, and rituals in the xenic realm.

Certain traumas are so hard to unravel that one may need to grapple with them by entering their own subconscious with the **Dive** [[Rituals#Dive|ritual]].

## By Accident

Maybe the most important point in this section, many mortals who end up in the xenic plane do so by accident! Sometimes portals are disguised as doorways, arches, cave entrances, or even something as unexpected as a portal in the back of a wardrobe.

# What You Might Find

Since the xeon itself exists both realms at once the xenic plane ends up reflecting the physical one in a lot of important ways. You might find valleys, mountains, gulches, rivers and streams, little towns and massive cities, tall trees and vast plains.

The differences lie in a couple different aspects:

1. The xeon is influenced by the minds of mortals and spirits, so the spiritual realm is touched by daydreams and nightmares, imagination and visualization.
2. Spirits that normally would be invisible in the physical realm are visible and active in the spiritual one.
3. Billions of years of shades (the spirits of dead mortal people, beasts, and plants) reside in the the xenic realm.
4. Powerful spirits such as gods and their servants actively shape the spiritual landscape around them, so it often reflects their needs, values, and vision.
5. Space and time don't act like they do in the physical realm
	- Time can stretch and shift, with long periods of time feeling short and moments lengthening to minutes or even hours
	- Places that at first appear far apart end up right next to one another, and you can land right where you started even after travelling a long distance

As far as inspiration for how the spirit world should feel and work think of the spirit world in Avatar the Last Airbender, the dreamworlds in The Boy and the Heron and Inception, landscape and creature interactions in Scavenger's Reign, and spirits and their rules in Princess Mononoke and other Ghibli films.

## Landscape

The environment in the xenic realm often mirrors the physical one, but since the xenic plane is a dreamworld of sorts that landscape is exaggerated and vivid. Where there's a forest in the physical realm massive red and purple trees loom in the xenic one, where there's a sea green waves tower under glowering maroon storms, where there's a city abstract metallic structures twist about one another, creating a maze of metal vines and thorns.

The xenic realm is saturated with psychedelic visuals, illogical features, intense colors, daunting proportions. Where grass should be prickly it could be soft and velvety, the bark of a tree rubbery, the ground itself could shed papery flakes that drift slowly upward.

Rivers flow backwards; rain seeps from deep underground and flies into the clouds above; vines grow quickly and then recede in pulses; day turns into night and into day in a blink, then spends hours in twilight: the laws of physics are remembered but not respected, and regular texture, smell, and taste is mere suggestion.

Traversing the spirit world is difficult, necessitating a belief that one can do things that break their own understanding of their capabilities. Gaping chasms must be leapt across, sky-scraping mountains must be scaled, expansive seas must be sailed, endless swamps must be trudged through.

Unlike in the physical realm, where sinking to the bottom of a lake, falling down a crevasse, or tumbling down a mountain will get you killed, failure in the spirit realm leads to interesting detours instead of dead ends. You might lose some time, take some damage, or get lost, but your path will keep winding, leading you to unexpected opportunities and unseen vistas.

Occasionally you'll see a holy site—an overgrown shrine to a powerful elemental; a gray, curling tree frequented by peaceful shades; a soaring temple devoted to an Archon; or a shimmering pool surrounded by gleaming sprites. These places can appear on the horizon or can be stumbled upon entirely by accident. Holy sites are places of peace and healing, where your minds find tranquility and resting mends your wounds (amount healed is determined by the GM).

# Hoa, The Universe Tree

When you enter the xenic plane from space you will see the Universe Tree, Hoa, streaming from the center of the supercluster. It's red and blue and beautiful, with gleaming orange leaves of flame in the summer and blue and purple and green fruits. Its gray bark covers most of it, but the newest branches are bright and fiery, and the oldest ones a dark blue.

If you enter within a current you will be surrounded by its branches, and will be able to see the great trunk of the center of the current you're in. It's possible to enter the great veins of the tree and travel that way, but there's no guarantee you won't get burned or frozen to death or blinded or crushed by the tree itself, or eaten by one of the many spirit beasts that live on the great bark of the Spirit Tree.

There are no roots; instead it's a cluster of trunks all coming from the Center, bursting from whatever energy source that exists at the core of the supercluster. Space is compacted around the Universe Tree, making it easier to travel from place to place, but also it can be dangerous as all sorts of spirits and gods travel through it, and it can be easier to spot travelers. If one wants to avoid suspicion they should enter one of the fruit of the tree or leave the xenic plane altogether.

The great fruit of the Spirit Tree are habited planets, and the fruit grow with the dreams and emotions of those who live there, whether they be mortal or spiritual. The Universe Tree is filled with an unimaginable number of fruit, but the larger ones are generally inhabited by mortals.

Sometimes, when the mortals have angered the spirits or unbalanced the xeon, the fruit will begin to rot. Valda is a great putrid fruit, massive but horribly diseased, infecting the tree around it.

Some branches are dead, laden by so many dead fruit that they can no longer be sustained. Some branches are infected with a darkness that moves through the tree, travelling, hunting, devouring.

As the xenic seasons ebb and flow, morphing from summer to fall to winter to spring again, the tree itself sings with color, leaves shimmering with the color of the season, orange in the summer, gray and dying in the fall, gone in the winter, and green again in the spring. The tree itself also responds to the season, the red pronounced in the summer and the blue in the winter.

In the center of the tree, protected by all the sprouting trunks, is a great light that is only sometimes visible from the branches. The trunks wrap around it tightly, keeping it hidden.

Other names for the Universe Tree:

- Hoa
- The Spirit Tree
- The Current Tree
- The Great Red Tree
- Uyal'gaiga (pbosh name)

# Pocket Planes

Over the eons gods and other powerful spirits have learned how to stretch and compress the xeon, impose their own will upon it, and ultimately create their own worlds with it.

These are called pocket planes, and as such have their own rules that live as reflections of the thoughts and desires of their creators.

They can be found anywhere: folded within the petals of a flower; deep under the ground, kept safe by the curling roots of a great tree; or as a pinprick of light high in the sky, an illusion of a star meant to conceal the work of a god. These realms can even be totally invisible, hiding in the folds of the xenic plane itself, visible only to a person with eyes to see.

These pocket dimensions are sometimes created to hide away secret knowledge, a powerful weapon, or to contain a being bound for great crimes.

They can be locked by a code or protected by powerful spirits; guarded by impenetrable barriers and walls of stone; or hidden at the center of a massive maze.

## Domains

All gods have their domain, which is a special pocket plane where they have ultimate power: they set the rules, they shape the landscape to look and function as they see fit, they use it to house their attendants, soldiers, or intermediaries.

A god cannot be hurt and has a near-ultimate level of power while they are within their domain, and they decide who can enter and who can't with impunity. Some gods will set their domain in one location permanently, while others will move them around constantly (though it takes some times for a domain to reach full power and security after a move).

It's usually possible to notice when one enters a domain if you have finely-tuned xenic senses unless the god has spent time and effort concealing the boundaries of their domain.

## Spirit Prisons

When the gods want to trap a being, bind a powerful spirit, or detain a devil they put them in a xenic prison. Here they are sealed by powerful enchantments or even guarded by beings loyal to the one doing the imprisoning.

Both good and bad gods imprison some of their most feared adversaries and criminals in the xenic plane.

These prisons can be as hard to find as any other pocket plane, but the more powerful the spirit being contained the more potent the magic used to contain them, and the stronger the illusions required to keep them hidden.

## Dreamweavers

Over the years there have been a handful of physics and other magicians that have delved deep into the xenic plane and studied its secrets. They've learned its rules, watched as the gods warp it on command, and observed how it shapes itself to anybody who walks its paths.

Long ago they learned that with a lot of practice, study, and discipline even mortals can grasp and shape it, train the xeon to their own will, and in so doing created the field of dreamweaving.

These dreamweavers found that manipulating the spirit world around them often offended the local spirits, so more often they would create their own pocket planes, their own little worlds to fill, mold, and destroy as they desired. Some dreamweavers were merciful little gods, using their powers to create beautiful worlds, utopic paradises, though these endeavors never lasted for long. Others were tyrants, creating simulacra to abuse and systems that favored only themselves.

On your travels you might encounter a dreamweaver or one of their abandoned worlds; be wary, for these people are never truly to be trusted, and their worlds often carry their own twisted rules.

# Cross-Plane Influence

The xenic plane is tied to all the xenic influences around it in the physical realm.

For example, in the peaceful, beautiful places of Ydilik the xenic plane is beautiful there as well, tended to by gentle spirits and peppered with shrines to the gods.

Valda's xenic plane, on the other hand, is a bleak and desolate wasteland, a distorted, upside-down version of Valda's vast cities. The imagination of the people has been ruined, their hopes and dreams trampled, so the spirit world is even drearier than the physical one.

This area is devoid of plant life, so there are no plant spirits to influence liven up xenic realm. The people consume so much xeon with their technology that the spirit world has responded, becoming ugly and inhospitable. Their disrespect of the elements themselves warp elementals into burgeoning monstrosities and make the weather in the physical realm unpredictable and intense.

The spirit world on Valda is populated by devils generated by the misery of the people, demons attracted by all the pain, and discontented shades with nowhere to go. Spirits don't show up much in the physical realm on Valda either because of all this disrespect of the spirit world and the xeon itself.

> [!NOTE] Time Dilation
> In physical places where time is dilated, such as the area around a black hole or in an town affected by a strong magical seal, the effect is _amplified_ in the xenic plane, making seconds into years and eons into moments.

## Xeon Consumption and the Spirit World

Tremendous and irresponsible consumption of xeon will annihilate any natural xenic growth of the spiritual plane and manifest as all sorts of nasty environmental features: big portals become a great horrific pit in the sky, a weather-controlling tower will become a great pillar of fire, sprawling webs of runes made to protect an area become a web of jet-black chains in the sky.

The xenic plane remains healthy by tending to the physical one: strong plant growth; a happy, content population of mortals, animals, and spirits; and a strong respect of the spirit world shown by building shrines, giving offerings, and listening to the spirts' needs all help the spirit world flourish and bring it closer to the physical one.

Great xeon channeling does not always damage the xenic plane, as the gods do it all the time without spoiling the spiritual aspect of the world. The difference is this: the gods have learned how to channel the xeon _in harmony_ with the spiritual world around them. They don't convert xeon with no respect to where it's coming from, the xeon they convert doesn't leak out into the world around them, and they have much more control over their powers than the mortals do over their technologies and magics.

## The Xenic Realm Affects the Physical One

At times the spirit world overflows: a devil gets strong enough to enter the physical plane, a god incites an incursion upon the mortal world, a powerful ritual rips the fabric of the xenic plane itself. The spirit realm itself may go wild and take portions of the physical world to itself, making the flora and fauna grow wilder.

Wielding great power in the xenic plane has a higher potential to destabilize the balance of both realms—dreamweavers and other strong beings must take care as to not cause reality to spiral out of control with their meddling, and if they're caught they might be bound for an age or even unmade.

## Dual Locations

Certain places such as shrines and other holy sites exist in both realms at once, enabling spirits to manifest in the physical realm and mortals to travel between worlds easily.

The most common dual locations are holy sites but they can also be darker or more surreal: a dark god draws the realms together to create a xenic maelstrom, a bloody battlefield fills with shades and devils that attack the local mortals, or lay lines tangle together and create a wild spirit jungle.

# Intro

A spark of vibrant purple in a pitch-dark room.

It blazes in the air for a second, sputtering, flaming tongues of lilac dripping like raindrops onto the floor of the glass container they're contained it, sparking in bright red and blue.

It lights up again, burbling this time, surface like an undulating granite, dropping tiny smooth violet stones that plink as they hit the floor. This time it doesn't burn out, it grows and grows, illuminating heavy stone carved walls, hefty copper cables running into the base of the container it floats in, and two figures dressed in thick white and gray robes, heads covered in black glass-like face shields.

A row of runes on the top of the plinth that holds the glass container aloft begin to glow red and blue as the purple strengthens, hardening from the burble into a shifting crystal, shimmering, facets collapsing into each other and reforming.

The purple prism starts pulsing slowly, and one of the figures flips a couple of heavy switches on a massive machine next to them; it grumbles to life slowly, dials illuminating and status indicators flashing. Wisps of purple mist begin to flow from the energy crystal and into the copper tubes.

The two figures raise their fists and shake them with elation, and then clasp each other in a hug, clipboards still in hand, faces and bodies still covered by their masks and cloaks.

# Basic Xeonics

Xeon is the basic spiritual substance of the universe, and is tied intimately with both the physics and the metaphysics of the XRU.

It is both a substance and an energy, collectable and discrete when contained and flowing and free-mixing in the natural world.

<span style="color: #999999;">Gray</span> xeon is everywhere, touching every space in between, across the seas and in the wind. It exists in the near-vacuum of space, reaching between stars and planets and moons, connecting distant spaces. It is ever-linking, thinking slowly, ponderously, dividing and subdividing and collecting bits of itself into shards of understanding.

<span style="color:#83cc83">Green</span> arises where exists the living, and it breathes and lives with them. It flourishes in the wildest places, in the depths of nebulas and on jungle planets, it grows and helps grow. It is the deep sense that lives in the body, the creative thoughts that arises from nowhere, the muse that resides in the animal subconscious.

<span style="color:#f75454">Red</span> is the heat, the passion that strikes through it all, the sun, burning bright and fueling the universe. It drives, it cleaves, it burns and purges. It leads and warms the soul, ever looking forward.

<span style="color:#5c54f7">Blue</span> is the steady ground, the law and binding of gravity, the one with an eye to procedure. It is tradition, it changes slowly, as a mountain does, but is never locked exactly in place. It is a sturdy shield, an ever-resent protection.

And below is how each color of xeon looks and feels.

	- Red is vibrant, full of energy and movement. It can look like lasers, sparks, little flames (though it'll always move and feel a bit different than a real flame), even kinda like lava.

	- Feels and looks solid, sturdy, and hard.
- <span style="color:#83cc83">Green</span>
	- Green is lively and sometimes even unpredictable. It can look like plants, like pollen, leaves, or vines; like animals or animal-like shapes; anything else living, such as a fungus or slime mold; or a haze or aura.
	- Feels and looks like the creativity, energy, and liveliness of life itself.
- <span style="color: #999999;">Gray</span>
	- Gray is calculating, precise. It can look like connected nodes, runes, straight lines and geometry, or even neuron-like shapes of gray, white, or black. Can also look a bit like water or wind but that's a bit more uncommon.

- Aeon (white)
	- White, or aeon, is all colors of xeon combined into one. It can look like any and all of the colors up above but combined, white with all sorts of rainbow colors swirling and sparking out of the white.

- Menon (black)
	- Black, or menon, is corrupted xeon. It can look like any of the basic colors of xeon but nasty, with black or even dark purples, dark reds, dark oranges and blues mixed in, and moves like sludge; grasping claws and teeth; red, bloodshot eyes; or a black, vicious flame.
	- Has a gross vibe, feels unnatural and twisted.

# Utilizing the Xeon

Mortals have figured out many different ways to harness with the xeon in order to achieve their goals, each of which carry with them distinct advantages and disadvantages.

The oldest and least complex method is to channel the xeon through your body, granting yourself powers. Most mortal species (the ones that can get powers at all) have had this method for most of their existence.

This method, that of the adepts, is simple but not easy, for it requires endless hours of training of the body, mind, and spirit to channel the xeon in a way that is controllable and won't completely destroy the wielder. Many xenic effects are possible through this method but overall adepts are much more limited than most other people, especially because even mastering _one_ power sometimes takes years (click [[Full Class Reference List#Adepts|here]] for adept types and their powers).

Magic and technology are about the same age and can both be very, very complex depending on how old and developed the tradition you're pulling from is.

## Magic

The magic that the physics use was discovered, not created: some group of people eons ago had "trained" the xeon over hundreds of thousands of years, teaching it to respond to certain words, then showing it what colors should be associated with what words, and then developing conjunctions and other relational words to enable the magician to control the distance, location, duration, and shape of the spell.

After all those eons the rtolka deciphered the original magic words from Ancient One carvings and started learning the language of the xeon.

There are other types of magic, however, such as the magic of the enchanters, which requires the user to have the right xenic substance to cast any spell but allows them to cast spells much more chaotic and whimsical than those of the physics, or that of alchemists, which uses some of the same substances but puts them into mixable potions that achieve arguably just as much chaos as the enchanters do.

Possessors use mostly the same magic as physics but instead of using and coloring the ambient xeon they harness the power of the spirits of mortals who have passed on. This grants them much more power and access to spells of possession and control at the cost of their morality.

Bards use magic developed by the kaldi: they use words like physics do but add song, dance, or even other types of visuals to manipulate people's minds and emotions. It is unknown exactly how they developed this magic, but the consensus is that they just happened into learning a handful of xenic words and made do the best they could.

## Technology

Modern tech, or ferrotech, generally uses copper wiring, silicon semiconductors, and zirconium transmitters (an a whole host of other xenic substances) and receivers to control robotics, send messages and connect to the gray web, and use and control all sorts of other commodities and doodads.

Another type of technology called magitech uses magical runes to enchant or imbue an item made or inset with xenic substances, creating some of the same effects as modern tech but also opening up a massive variety of effects inaccessible to ferrotech.

The technology of the rtolka, called retrotech, splits the difference a bit, using runes to channel xeon through tubes instead of conducting it through copper. This makes it possible to use retrotech to channel the xeon into ritual circles and other magic-based applications, though it's not capable of some of the more whimsical effects that magitech is. Retrotech got its name because it resembles more archaic technology of humans' past—if you can't tell, humans think and act everything revolves around them.

Some more rare types of technology are geotech, the rune-focused tech of the oro, phytotech, plant technology used by the uthali of Jiyyagu, and zoatech, flesh technology used by the hualaki, also of Jiyyagu.

## Pacts

Another way to wield the xeon is through a pact, such as one with a god, a devil, or a titan. Making a pact makes one a mage and gives them access to powers that nobody else does, such as massive heals and buffs (for clerics pacted to light gods), health-draining or self-destructive damage (for warlocks pacted to dark gods), all sorts of buffs and debuffs (for sorcerers pacted to devils), or turning one's body into elements such as fire and water (for demititans pacted to titans).

## Spiritualism

A very unique way to harness the xeon is to make relationships with all sorts of xenic spirits, whether they be the souls of dead spirits or animals, elementals, or light and dark spirits to buff and debuff their allies, control the elements, or perform a wide array of utility.

The spirits around mystics can "latch" onto their targets, helping or hurting them over a period of time; summoners solidify their spirits to give them more intense damage at the cost of making them able to take damage; and elementalists team up with a bunch of xenic spirits that enable them to manipulate the elements around them.

# Advanced Xeonics

## Xenic Interactions

![[Xenon Conversions v2.png|502]]

Each color of xeon interacts with the other colors in distinct ways; some of them repel one another, some work well together, and some are easy to convert from one to another, some are _very_ hard.

The easier the conversion is the more one color naturally "wants" to move to the other color. The harder the conversion the rarer it is to naturally change.

- <span style="color:#f75454">Red</span> likes to expand, fights and doesn't combine well with blue, and is made easily from gray and pretty easily from green.
- <span style="color:#5c54f7">Blue</span> likes to condense, resists and doesn't combine well with red, and is made easily from gray and pretty easily from green.
- <span style="color:#83cc83">Green</span> moves toward places of equilibrium (not too hot or too cold), is made easily from red and blue, and isn't made easily from gray.
- <span style="color: #999999;">Gray</span> interconnects into webs, is made easily from red and blue, and is made pretty easily from green.
- Aeon (white) is a combination of all colors of xeon above, and is _very_ hard to create.
- Menon (black) is corrupted xeon of any color, and is _also_ very hard to create.

There are no recorded instances of aeon being changed to any other type of xeon. Menon cannot be converted to aeon, and menon doesn't naturally change to any other type of xeon.

## Xeon as a Spiritual Substance

Xeon is the fundamental creationary substance, as it's what the gods use to create and destroy stars, planets, and life forms. The xeon itself is also, to a certain extent, alive and intelligent, an indestructible substance that makes up all spirits, whether they be mortal or elemental. The process of making a spirit is not well understood, and the xeon that makes up spirits is fine enough that it doesn't show up on xenic sensors.

### The Xenic Centers

Each mortal has one xenic center for each color of xeon, which is where that color builds up in the body, and where xeon of that type is anchored when that person channels the xeon with powers or through xenic substances.

The <span style="color: #999999;">gray</span> xenic center is at the very top of the head.
The <span style="color:#83cc83">green</span> xenic center is at the base of the skull, where the neck meets the head in the back.
The <span style="color:#f75454">red</span> xenic center is right where the heart of the mortal is (differs per mortal).
The <span style="color:#5c54f7">blue</span> xenic center is right at the center of gravity.

### The Three Taboos

There are three things that have been forbidden by the gods, things that if you do (at least without their permission and/or help) will destroy your soul.

1. Create mortal souls
2. Gain immortality
3. Seek absolute power over the xeon

Not coincidentally, having the capacity for these three things is what makes a god (though most gods technically only create mortal bodies, not the souls that inhabit them).

## Xenic Microphysics

Each xenic particle (which acts both like a physical thing and as a wave) has a certain charge (e.g. R04 G43 B21). It's charged a certain amount with each color, and the particular proportion of charges it has determines its particular color.

When it comes in contact with physical things (such as stars, planets, life, gems, or life forms that can charge it at will) it becomes colored because of that interaction, at which point the particle of xeon both gains different characteristics and starts influencing the xeon around it (its color starts rubbing off on the particles around it).

A particle is gray when it's colored more or less same in all colors. (e.g. R40 G40 B40, or R33 G32.5 B33.23) This happens because if there's nothing around to color it, all the particles' charges will rub off on one other and eventually it'll come to a place of equilibrium. Outer space is the most gray of all places, especially when there's nothing else around for lightyears. Air above a planet and within water and other liquids are also pretty good places for gray xeon, provided that their temperatures aren't too extreme.

> [!NOTE] Example
> Charge is just how much color it has; say you have two particles:
>
> Particle1: R50 G00 B00
> Particle2: R05 G00 B00
>
> These two particles have the same color but different charges. So if Particle2 was charged more, it would enact the same effect as Particle1 would.
>
> Both are red, but their capacities to effect the world are very different.

### Charges

Distinct charge proportions will produce certain effects: running the xeon through a ruby starts putting off heat, or through a sapphire starts putting off cold, etc. There are many other substances that achieve different effects, and all this comes down to the exact proportions of charge that it requires.

The xeon is usually invisible when the charge isn't strong, and it usually becomes visible when it's charged.

#### Aeon and Menon

Aeon contains extremely high, fairly-equal charge (and also some other characteristics that suggest that it is fundamentally different from other types of xeon), and menon contains what is considered negative charge (though basically nothing else is known about it from a qualitative standpoint since it's so hard to capture and do tests on, and since creating it usually entails doing something heinous).

Aeon and menon are about equally hard to contain and test, so not much data has been gathered about either of them.

There is a theory that spirits are made of uncharged aeon, which doesn't make sense if aeon is just highly-charged xeon, but since spirits don't show up on any xenic sensors (and a large amount xenic scientists are skeptical about the idea of spirits to begin with, _and_ only certain people can see spirits at all), it's literally impossible to verify this claim with the instruments at hand.

### Achieving Different Effects

Certain people can achieve the same effects as some of these gems by channeling xeon through their bodies, but how it isn't clear why some people can achieve these effects while others can't.

Some xenic effects can't be achieved by technological means, and others can't by magical means; something about the substances that you would use for a given effect rejects either a technological or magical approach, but it's not yet known why that is. ## The Xenic Seasons

For unknown reasons there is a season cycle that exists throughout the entire Isveh Supercluster. There's a winter, where <span style="color:#5c54f7">blue</span> xeon is marginally stronger, a spring, which strengthens <span style="color:#83cc83">green</span>, a summer, which makes <span style="color:#f75454">red</span> just a bit more powerful, and a fall, which sharpens <span style="color: #999999;">gray</span>.

These changes are very subtle, mostly affecting the macro: how much red xeon does it take to warprun from one place to another, how much does a jungle flourishes or how much food can be produced on a large scale, or how quick can a system of computers crunch numbers? These seasonal xenic effects are so subtle that on most planets and other places that already have strong xenic signatures (such as next to a star or in a flourishing nebula) the strengthening is barely felt.

The biggest changes that the seasons affect are xenic weather features! Sometimes in summer red lay lines turn from their customary invisible into a shimmering tongue of flame in the sky, in spring tiny green motes can appear in a forest, in winter the mountains can begin a deep hum as blue courses through them.

So keep it in mind if you're traveling! They can turn a peaceful journey into an eventful one.

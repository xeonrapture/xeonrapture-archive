# Basic Details

Name pronounced "ruh-toll-kuh"

- Common Chroma: Gray
- Rarity: Common
- Native Planet: Rtolka-Dala
- Created By: Rtolka, God of Knowledge
- Creation Date: 50,188 PAE (Pre-Amethyst Age)

# Physical Description

Long thin bodies, four spindly legs, long arms, bright or deep red skin that gets paler as they get older. Two whiteless eyes that are usually dark blue, black, or white. Feet and legs are perfect for clambering over bookshelves, rocks, or other shifty surfaces (their buildings are usually pretty lateral because of this). No hair!

They live for about 200 years. 

Pretty much all rtolka are on the ace spectrum, and because of this they have a pretty low fertility rate so there aren't as many rtolka across the supercluster as other common mortal species.

# <abbr title="These personality traits are generalizations, and better describe the stereotype about this species than the species' temperament itself. Each mortal species contains a huge range of personalities and motivations.">Personality Traits</abbr>

Much of their drive is connected with a need to understand. They tend to be extremely intelligent, reserved natural scholars, no matter what they end up doing. Very intense and dedicated to the truth and to exactness. At worst they can get prickly and vengeful when they feel intellectually threatened, and at best are willing to hear all sides of an argument, weigh all the data, and create new ways of understanding the universe.

All about gathering knowledge. Often have latent mind-quickening xeon powers, so them and their culture are almost impossible to keep up with for other species.

# Known For

Known for their extensive physical and digital libraries, being the first archonic species to discover the xenic language and develop magic, and for creating [[Xeon#Technology|retrotech]].

In their culture they have a tradition called "respect for the dissent", which is just what it sounds like: a practice where they emphasize listening to the dissenting opinion, sometimes to the point of ignoring the status quo opinion completely. It keeps their knowledge fresh and true, always adapting to new circumstances and recognizing hard facts, but is also sometimes taken too far, keeping them mired in a consistent reorienting and reevaluating.

Their homeworld planet is in the same solar system as the pbosh's homeworld, and their inter-species grudge is extensive.
